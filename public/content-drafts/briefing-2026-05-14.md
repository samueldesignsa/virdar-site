## One Insight

The roofing walk-in has a specific advantage: the opener is completely non-threatening. You're not walking in and saying "your sales process is broken." You're asking one question: "What happens to estimates you send out when a homeowner goes quiet after two weeks?"

Every roofing owner knows exactly what happens. Nothing. The estimate file sits there. Maybe someone remembers to call. Usually they don't. The owner already knows this is a problem — they live it every time a storm generates 30 leads and they close 8.

You're not diagnosing a problem they don't know they have. You're naming one they think about constantly. That's a fundamentally easier conversation than walking into a dental office and explaining why their recall process matters.

The math is also self-evident: estimate volume × close rate × average job size. If a roofer does $1.5M a year and closes 28% of estimates, a 5-point close rate improvement is worth $150,000+ in additional bookings. Most owners can do that math in their head while you're still explaining it.
