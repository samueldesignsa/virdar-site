# Walk-In Research — Friday, May 15, 2026

**Zone: Plano — Legacy Drive / Preston Road corridor (West Plano professional park) + Central/East Plano service strip**

Why Plano today: Plano has two distinct business zones worth hitting back-to-back. The Legacy Drive / Independence Pkwy corridor is dense with independent insurance agencies, financial advisors, and medical offices. The Jupiter Road / K Avenue / 15th Street zone (East Plano) runs long on auto repair, dental, and local service businesses. Both are within 10 minutes of each other. Friday morning is strong here — business owners are wrapping up the week and often have more mental bandwidth than Monday.

## Target 1: Independent Insurance Agency — Legacy / Preston Road Area

**What they do:** Independent P&C agency — auto, home, commercial lines. Not a captive (not State Farm, Farmers, Allstate). Look for local agency names.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "independent insurance agency Plano TX Legacy" or search along Legacy Drive between Preston Rd and the Tollway. There are clusters of small offices in the strip centers along Legacy Dr and Warren Pkwy. Also check the office parks just south of Legacy near Independence Pkwy.

**What to notice:** Most independent Plano agencies don't have structured renewal outreach. Plano's residential growth over the past 8 years means a lot of these agencies added hundreds of clients fast — and now have a back-book of customers who've never heard from them except at renewal notice time. Review pages on Google often show a flurry of reviews at launch, then nothing recent.

**Hook:** "Independent agencies in Plano tend to have a specific problem — clients who joined during the housing growth years and have never been proactively contacted between renewals. I'm curious: in the 45 days before a policy renews, what does your outreach process look like?"

**Follow-up math:** A 400-policy book with 12% annual lapse rate that improves to 8% through systematic renewal outreach retains 16 additional clients per year. At $1,200 average annual premium, that's $19,000 in preserved revenue from the existing book.

**Best time:** 9:30–11am. After the morning email rush, before lunch.

## Target 2: Independent Financial Advisor or RIA Firm — Legacy Business Park

**What they do:** Fee-only financial planning, wealth management, or investment advisory for affluent clients. Plano/West Plano demographic skews high-income — this market has a strong independent RIA presence.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "independent financial advisor Plano TX" or "RIA firm Plano TX Legacy." Filter for small firms (not Edward Jones branch offices, not Merrill Lynch). Look for firms with 2–5 advisors, local name, been open 5+ years. The Legacy Business Park area and the office parks near Independence Pkwy and Warren Pkwy are good hunting grounds.

**What to notice:** Most independent RIA firms run their client relationship management manually — birthday calls logged in spreadsheets, annual review scheduling done by hand, referral requests made awkwardly in conversation or not at all. The advisors with strong practices also have the worst admin processes because they've grown on referrals and never built systems.

**Hook:** "Financial advisors I talk to are usually great at the client relationship — and terrible at the systematic touchpoints that protect that relationship. Annual reviews that get scheduled late, referral requests never made, clients who drift away because no one was tracking their life events. I'm curious what your process looks like for the in-between moments — not the big review meetings, but the 8 months in between them."

**Revenue math:** An advisor with 80 clients who runs consistent mid-year check-ins and systematic referral asks at the right moments generates 4–6 additional referral introductions per year. At the average fee for a new advisory relationship, that's $20K–$40K in new AUM-based revenue from an outreach process the client already expects.

**Best time:** 9–11am. Financial advisors often have lighter morning schedules without client appointments.

## Target 3: Independent Auto Repair Shop — Jupiter Road / Central Plano

**What they do:** General auto repair — diagnostics, oil changes, brakes, tires, scheduled maintenance. Independent, not Firestone, Midas, or Jiffy Lube.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "independent auto repair Plano TX" or "auto shop Jupiter Road Plano." The Jupiter Rd corridor between Park Blvd and Spring Creek Pkwy has a strong concentration of independent shops. Also check along K Avenue and 15th Street in older Plano commercial strips.

**What to notice:** Most independent shops have no declined service follow-up. The customer who declines a brake job in March and drives away is never contacted again. Also check: Google review response patterns. Shops with 4.0–4.3 stars often have a handful of negative reviews they've never responded to — that's a visible gap any owner knows is hurting them.

**Hook:** "Auto shops have a specific follow-up problem that most owners I talk to don't have a system for: declined services. When a customer declines brakes or tires and leaves, does anyone reach out two weeks later when they've had time to think about it? Most shops say that depends on whether someone remembers. That's the gap."

**Revenue math:** A shop declining 10 services per week at $350 average that converts 20% of those to a follow-up appointment recovers $36,400 per year from jobs they already diagnosed and estimated.

**Best time:** 8–10am. Shop owners and service managers are in before the bays fill. After 10am it gets chaotic.

## Target 4: Independent Dental Practice — Plano Strip Centers

**What they do:** General dentistry — cleanings, restorations, cosmetic, orthodontics. Independent practice, not Aspen Dental or a large DSO.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "independent dentist Plano TX" or "family dental Plano." Look for single-location practices with 50–150 reviews, a local or doctor-name branding. Strip centers along Preston Rd, Legacy Dr, and Park Blvd all have dental offices.

**What to notice:** Recall gap. Dental practices typically lose 20–30% of their active patient base every year to lapsed recall — patients due for a cleaning who never scheduled. Most practices run one automated reminder, then nothing. The patient who doesn't book after the first reminder gets zero follow-up.

**Hook:** "Dental practices have a recall problem that's bigger than most owners realize because the lapsed patients are invisible — they're not cancelling, they're just not coming back. I'm curious: for patients who are 14+ months overdue for a cleaning and didn't respond to the standard reminder, does your practice have a second or third outreach, or does that patient just age out?"

**Revenue math:** A practice with 600 active patients and 75% return rate that improves to 83% through systematic multi-touch recall recovers 48 additional appointments per year. At $210 average hygiene visit, that's $10,000 in revenue from patients the practice already acquired.

**Best time:** 10am–12pm. Dental practices have a mid-morning window between early hygiene appointments and the lunch hour.

## Target 5: Roofing Contractor — Plano / North Plano

**What they do:** Residential and commercial roofing installation, repair, and insurance claim work.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "roofing contractor Plano TX" or "roofing company North Plano." Look for independently owned contractors with 30–120 reviews, trucks with Plano/Collin County branding, 4+ years in business. Avoid storm-chaser operations (those with 3-star averages and complaints in reviews about bait-and-switch on insurance claims). You want the owner-operated local shop that's been building a reputation.

**What to notice:** This is peak season. A roofing contractor right now has more leads than their phone can handle cleanly. That means dropped follow-up on estimates, insurance claim status questions going unanswered, and homeowners who went quiet after the initial meeting and signed with someone else. The pain is fresh.

**Hook:** "It's hail season, which means you're probably getting more leads than your office can cleanly follow up on. I'm curious — what's your process for estimates that go out and then go quiet after two weeks? Do you have a follow-up sequence, or does it depend on someone having time to call?"

**Revenue math:** A roofing company doing 40 estimates per month at $11,000 average job and 28% close rate that improves close rate by 5 points through systematic follow-up closes 2 additional jobs per month. That's $22,000/month, $264,000/year in additional revenue.

## Target 6: Med Spa / Aesthetic Clinic — Legacy / Shops at Legacy Area

**What they do:** Injectables (neurotoxin, filler), laser treatments, body contouring, facials. Independent practice or small multi-location group. Not a franchise.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "med spa Plano TX" or "aesthetic clinic Legacy Plano." The Shops at Legacy and the West Plano corridor have several independently owned aesthetics practices. Look for 50–120 reviews, 4.5+ rating, been open 3+ years.

**What to notice:** Rebooking drop-off. Neurotoxin clients should return every 3–4 months. Filler clients every 6–9 months. Most practices have no automated rebooking outreach — they rely on clients to call when they're ready. The 90-day gap after the last appointment is when clients start drifting to competitors who are running ads.

**Hook:** "Aesthetic practices have a specific rebooking problem — clients who should come back every 12 weeks but don't because no one followed up at week 10. I'm curious: for clients whose last appointment was 90 days ago and who haven't rebooked, does your practice have an outreach running, or does it depend on them calling?"

**Revenue math:** A med spa with 250 active clients and 70% annual rebooking rate that improves to 83% through systematic outreach recovers 32 additional treatment visits per year. At $425 average treatment value, that's $13,600 in recovered annual revenue.

## Route

**Morning (8–10am):** Start in Central/East Plano — hit the auto repair and roofing targets first. Jupiter Rd area: park once, hit 2–3 shops within walking distance or a short drive.

**Mid-morning (10am–12pm):** Move west toward Legacy. Dental first (strip center on Preston or Legacy), then med spa (Shops at Legacy area or adjacent strip).

**Late morning (11am–1pm):** Insurance agency and financial advisor in Legacy Business Park — these targets work better when the office owner has gotten through their morning emails.

**Drive:** Plano is 25–30 minutes north of Dallas on US-75 or Central Expwy. Leave by 8am to hit the first shop by 8:30am.

## Zone Notes

- **Tone:** Plano business owners are data-aware and practical. Specific numbers land well — don't pitch abstract benefits, pitch a concrete dollar amount they can verify against their own numbers.
- **Friday energy:** Friday mornings in Plano tend to be efficient — owners are wrapping the week and thinking about next week. They'll give you 5 minutes if you open with something specific. They won't give you 30 if you're vague.
- **Parking:** Legacy area has ample lot parking. East Plano strip centers are easy in/out. No need to pre-plan parking.
- **Dress:** Business casual on the west side (Legacy/Preston). More relaxed on the east side (auto shops, roofing). Read the room.

## Opener That Works Anywhere

"I work with small businesses in this area on one specific problem: the follow-up that doesn't happen. Leads that go cold, clients who drift, services that get declined and never revisited. I'm not here to sell you software — I build custom systems. I just want to understand what your actual gap is and see if it's something worth fixing. Do you have five minutes?"
