## Target 1: Orthodontic Practice — Preston Center or University Park corridor

**What they do:** Independent orthodontic practice serving Highland Park and University Park families. HPISD starts August 13 — six weeks from today. Families with kids approaching the braces age (11–14) are actively thinking about treatment timing. Summer is the strongest window for treatment starts in orthodontics: no school conflicts, adjustment period before the school year, and a patient list that hasn't been touched by the practice since March.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "orthodontist Highland Park TX," "orthodontist University Park TX," or "orthodontist Preston Center Dallas." Owner-orthodontist practice. Not a Smile Doctors franchise. Not an orthodontic DSO. Look for a solo or two-doctor independent practice with 150–500 reviews and a name-brand orthodontist visible on the website.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- Consultation conversion: Any "schedule your free consultation" CTA on the website? Most orthodontic websites push consultations but don't visibly follow up with unconverted consults. Ask: "How many consults did you do this spring who haven't started treatment?"
- Summer scheduling messaging: Any back-to-school or "start before August" angle on their website or social? Almost certainly not. This is the gap.
- Online scheduling: Can a parent book a consultation from a phone at 10pm without calling? If not, that's a secondary hook.

**Hook (new blog post live today):**
"You probably have 25 to 40 families from consultations this spring who said they'd think about it and haven't started treatment. For a lot of those families, the reason they haven't scheduled is timing — summer is when they planned to do it. HPISD starts August 13. A message this week to your unconverted consults — 'a lot of families start treatment in summer, we have July mornings open, here are three dates' — brings in 15 to 20 of them before your August schedule fills up. At this practice's investment level, that's $85,000 to $110,000 in new starts from a list you already have. What does your post-consult follow-up look like right now?"

**Revenue math:**
- 35 unconverted consults from last 12 months; 55% respond to summer-specific outreach = 19 treatment starts
- DFW comprehensive orthodontic treatment: $5,500–$6,200 average
- 19 starts at $5,800 average = $110,200 from one outreach campaign
- Plus adjustment patients due for quarterly checks: 50 patients past window → 35 respond → $5,250 in adjustment revenue

## Target 2: Dental Practice — Northwest Highway or Lovers Lane corridor

**What they do:** Independent family or cosmetic dental practice in the Highland Park / University Park corridor. Park Cities dental practices serve one of the highest-spending dental demographics in DFW — average ticket for a hygiene visit + exam runs $275–$350 (vs. $185–$225 in the suburbs). These practices have been in the same locations for decades and have large, established patient lists they're not fully working.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "dentist Highland Park TX," "dentist University Park TX," or "dentist near Preston Center Dallas." Independent owner-dentist. Not Aspen Dental, not Bright Now, not a DSO. 150–600 reviews. Practices on Northwest Hwy, Lovers Lane, or in the Preston Center area.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- July 4 week: July 4 is 18 days away. A Park Cities practice with 90–100 appointments that week is looking at 20–25 last-minute cancellations. Same math Beverly used yesterday in Southlake.
- Back-to-school pediatric recall: HPISD starts August 13. Has the practice sent any outreach to overdue pediatric patients? Almost never.
- Patient loyalty vs. follow-up: Park Cities dental patients are loyal — the 18-year-old practice relationship is real. But loyalty doesn't mean they self-schedule. They respond to outreach they just don't receive.

**Hook (two options):**

*Option A — July 4 week (most urgent):*
"July 4 is 18 days away. You're probably looking at 20 to 25 last-minute cancellations that week — families finalizing vacation plans in the next week are going to cancel the Tuesday before they leave. The practices that protect that week send a message now: 'your appointment is confirmed, if plans change we have openings July 15 or 16.' That message drops holiday cancellations from a 24 percent rate to around 10 percent. At this practice's ticket, that's $5,000 to $7,000 in one week. What does your July 4 week cancellation rate typically look like?"

*Option B — Back-to-school recall:*
"HPISD starts August 13. You have families in your system — probably 100 to 140 patients — with kids overdue for their annual cleaning. They're scheduling this summer if you reach them. They'll call in August when your July mornings are already gone if you don't. A message to your overdue pediatric patients this week fills those slots. What does your proactive pediatric recall look like for summer?"

**Revenue math:**
- July 4 angle: 95 appointments scheduled June 30–July 3; 24% unmanaged vs. 10% managed cancellation = 13 recovered appointments at $310 Park Cities average = $4,030 in one week. Annual holiday management across six holidays = $24,000–$36,000 per provider per year.
- Back-to-school: 800 active patients, 130 overdue pediatric patients; 68% proactive vs. 33% passive response = 45 additional pediatric appointments at $250 = $11,250. Add parent same-visit conversion (18 adults at $320) = $5,760. Total: $17,010.

## Target 3: Med Spa or Aesthetic Clinic — Knox-Henderson or Preston Center

**What they do:** Independent medical aesthetics practice serving the Park Cities and inner Dallas demographic. Knox-Henderson has several independent med spas and aesthetic injectors. Preston Center has medical office buildings with solo-practitioner aesthetic MDs. Highland Park / UP patients have very high average tickets — Botox/filler in this zip code range runs $1,100–$1,400 per visit. A 200-client practice with a 25% quarterly lapse rate has 50 clients right now who haven't been in since late February.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "med spa Highland Park TX," "medical aesthetics Knox Henderson," "aesthetic clinic Preston Center Dallas." Independently owned or small-group practice — not a Medspa chain, not a laser clinic franchise. Owner-injector (MD, DO, NP, PA) visible on the website.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- Lapsed client messaging: Is there any re-engagement campaign visible on Instagram or email? Most independent Park Cities spas are not running systematic lapsed-client outreach. They rely on the client to rebook.
- Membership structure: VIP memberships are common in this market. But membership programs don't eliminate lapse — they reduce cost but clients still drift if not proactively prompted.
- Review volume vs. client base: A 5-year-old independent spa with 180 reviews and 200 active clients is producing 36 reviews per year, 3 per month. With 200 clients, they should see 15–25 reviews per month. The ask isn't happening at scale.

**Hook:** "Your client who got Botox and filler in February — she's at the four-month mark. She's been meaning to rebook. She's not gone, but she's waiting for her face to tell her to go back, not the practice. With 200 active clients and a typical 25% quarterly lapse rate, you have 50 clients in that window right now. A message this week — 'it's been four months, your areas are due, here are three openings next week' — brings back 28 to 35 of them. At this market's pricing, that's $30,000 to $49,000 from your existing list. What does your 90-day lapsed-client outreach look like?"

**Revenue math:** 200 active clients, 25% quarterly lapse = 50 clients; 60% reactivation = 30 clients returning at $1,250 Park Cities average = $37,500 from one campaign. Ongoing 90-day automated check-in reduces lapse rate from 25% to 11%, adding $30,000–$45,000 annually.

## Target 4: Financial Advisor or Wealth Management Firm — Preston Center

**What they do:** Independent registered investment advisor (RIA) or financial planning practice serving the Park Cities demographic. Preston Center has a high concentration of independent financial advisors and estate planning attorneys. The Park Cities client base is among the highest-AUM in DFW — $1M to $5M+ portfolios common. Mid-year review outreach is the same hook Beverly uses for suburban advisors, but the stakes are higher here.

**Where to look:** Google Maps or a quick search for "financial advisor Preston Center Dallas" or "wealth management Highland Park TX." Independent RIA or fee-only planning practice. Not Edward Jones, not Wells Fargo, not a wirehouse. Solo or two-advisor firm. The website should show a named advisor, credentials (CFP, CFA), and an independent firm name.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- Mid-year review communication: Any mention of proactive mid-year portfolio check-ins on the website? Almost none do this systematically.
- Referral program: Any visible "introduce a friend" or referral incentive program? Most independent advisors generate most new clients through referrals but don't have a systematic ask.
- Content marketing: Blog, newsletter, or LinkedIn presence suggesting they're actively communicating with their client base? Most aren't.

**Hook:** "Half the year is over. Your clients who haven't heard from you since their January review are now six months in without a check-in. For a 75-client practice, that's probably 40 to 50 clients who are overdue for a mid-year conversation. A message this week — 'half the year is done, I want to review your plan against where markets are now, I have 30-minute slots next week' — gets a 60 to 70 percent response rate from an established client base. The conversations that come out of those reviews also tend to generate the most referrals of the year. What does your mid-year outreach to existing clients look like?"

**Revenue math:** 75 clients, 50 overdue for mid-year check-in; 65% respond = 33 scheduled conversations. Mid-year conversations generate 3–5 referrals on average in Park Cities practices (vs. 1–2 in suburban markets, because referrals at this income level are to people with similar portfolios). 4 referrals at $800K average AUM at 1% advisory fee = $32,000 in new recurring annual revenue from one campaign.

## Target 5: Law Firm — Preston Center or Lovers Lane corridor

**What they do:** Solo or small firm attorney in family law, estate planning, personal injury, or business law serving the Park Cities clientele. Preston Center has a high concentration of boutique law practices — estate planning attorneys who handle Park Cities wealth, family law attorneys who handle Highland Park divorces, business attorneys who serve the small business owners in the corridor. These practices share the same "revenue stuck in the follow-up" problem as dental and chiro: past clients who need updated estate plans, ongoing family law matters, or business agreements that never get attention because nobody is tracking the follow-up.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "estate planning attorney Highland Park TX," "family law attorney Preston Center," or "business attorney University Park." Independently named firm. Solo or 2–4 attorney firm. Not a large firm. 50–200 reviews. These practices often have significant word-of-mouth and established reputation with minimal digital marketing.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- Estate plan update outreach: Any communication to past estate planning clients about updating wills and trusts after life events (new kids, retirement, divorce)? Almost never systematic.
- Client portal or document automation: Law firms lose significant billable hours to document chases. Any mention of client-facing document systems on the website?
- Review volume vs. case volume: An estate planning attorney who did 80 estate plans last year should have more than 30 Google reviews. The ask isn't happening.

**Hook:** "You probably did 60 to 90 estate plans in the last three years. Every one of those clients has a life event since then that should trigger a plan review — a new child, a death in the family, a business sold, a divorce, retirement. Most of them haven't heard from you since the plan was signed. A message to your estate planning clients — 'it's been [X] years, life changes usually mean your plan needs an update, we have 30-minute review slots available' — has a 45 to 55 percent response rate from established clients. At your billing rate, that's [X] in revision work from clients who are already yours. What does your past-client follow-up look like?"

**Revenue math:** 75 estate plan clients from last 3 years; 50% respond to review outreach = 38 review appointments; 70% identify a needed update at $1,200 average revision = $31,920 in billable work from existing clients. Plus 6–8 referrals from review conversations at Park Cities estate planning average ($3,500–$5,000 per new plan) = $21,000–$40,000 additional.

## Zone Notes

- **This is the highest-income walk-in market Beverly has worked.** Highland Park and University Park household incomes are $175,000 to $250,000+. The revenue math for every pitch is stronger here than anywhere else Beverly has worked this spring.
- **Expect more skepticism than the suburbs.** Park Cities business owners have been pitched by vendors for 20 years. The counter is specificity: not "we use AI to help your business," but "you have 35 unconverted consults from this spring and your summer scheduling window closes in 8 weeks." Specificity beats skepticism.
- **Long-tenure practices respond to loss math.** A dental practice that has been at the same location for 18 years isn't excited about new technology. They ARE concerned about the patients they're losing to practices that follow up better. Frame every pitch around what the business is currently losing, not what the system does.
- **HPISD starts August 13** — 8 weeks from today. Back-to-school hooks are timely but not urgent yet. The July 4 hook (18 days) is more urgent for dental practices.
- **Parking:** Preston Center has surface parking lots and the Preston Center shopping area. Knox-Henderson is street parking and small lots. University Park is residential with commercial strips — street parking available.
