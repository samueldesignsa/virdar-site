## Target 1: Independent RIA or Wealth Management Firm — Southlake

**Search:** "financial advisor Southlake TX" or "wealth management Southlake TX" on Google Maps — filter for independent practices. Look for:
- Independently branded office (not Edward Jones, Merrill Lynch, Ameriprise, Raymond James branch)
- Firm name includes a person's name or a geographic/thematic name ("Summit," "Legacy," "Parkway," "Northstar")
- 10–60 Google reviews, 4.6+ rating, been operating 5+ years
- Website that mentions "independent," "fee-only," or "fiduciary"

**What you'll likely notice:** No systematic referral process. Their Google listing has sporadic reviews going back years — not a consistent post-service request. Annual review scheduling is manual. The owner/advisor is doing all client contact personally with no support system.

**Walk-in approach:** These offices are often small — a receptionist or office manager, and 1 to 3 advisors. Ask to speak with the principal or owner. Not a service manager, not a junior advisor.

**Opener:** "I work with independent RIAs on one specific problem: the gap between how many clients they have and how many referrals they should be generating from them. Most practices I talk to get almost all their growth from referrals, but they don't have a systematic process for asking. I'm curious — when a client hits a financial milestone or you complete a plan, does your firm have a way to ask for an introduction at that exact moment?"

**Why this works:** It's not an insult to their business. It's a specific gap that every advisor recognizes in themselves. The follow-up: "The math on systematic referral timing for a 100-household practice at 1% AUM is around $48,000 in additional fee revenue per year from one additional client per quarter. That's the number I show advisors when we build this out."

**Hook from today's blog post:** Referral request timing. The milestone-triggered ask converts at 3–5x the rate of a generic request. For a Southlake advisor managing $100M–$200M AUM, one additional client per quarter from systematic referrals is $48,000–$96,000/year in added revenue. That's the headline.

**Best time:** 10am–noon. Advisors are between morning client calls.

## Target 2: Independent Financial Advisor or CFP — Colleyville

**Search:** "financial planner Colleyville TX" or "CFP Colleyville TX" — look for small independent offices along Glade Rd, Cheek-Sparger Rd, or Bedford Rd.

**What to look for:** Solo or 2-person practice. CFP or ChFC designation on the door or website. Practice that's been open 8+ years (they have the client base, they just haven't built the systems). Look for practices that mention "retirement planning" or "comprehensive financial planning" — these advisors have clients in or near retirement who need annual reviews and estate planning coordination, which are the highest-automation-leverage touchpoints.

**Alternate opener (if they seem skeptical of "AI"):** "I'm not here to sell you a chatbot or a robo-advisor product. I work with independent advisors on the logistics side — making sure 100% of your clients get a review conversation every year, that you're sending a referral request at the right moment after every milestone, and that prospects who said 'not yet' six months ago aren't falling out of the pipeline permanently. That's the piece most independent practices are missing, and it's fixable without hiring staff."

**Hook:** Annual review completion rate. An independent advisor with 80 clients who completes 65 reviews per year is leaving 15 conversations — and the planning opportunities inside them — on the table. Automation gets that number to 76–78 with a 45-day scheduling sequence that runs itself.

**Best time:** 10:30am–noon or 2–4pm.

## Target 3: Independent Insurance Agency — Southlake or Colleyville

**Search:** "insurance agency Southlake TX" or "independent insurance agent Colleyville TX" — look for independent agents (not State Farm, Allstate, or Farmers captive agents). Search for "independent insurance" specifically to filter.

**What to look for:** Agency with 3–10 staff, handling personal lines (home, auto, umbrella) and small commercial accounts. They have a policy renewal calendar that should be working automatically but isn't. Policy anniversaries are perfectly predictable. Most agencies still rely on the carrier to send the renewal notice and the client to call in — which is how clients discover it's time to shop.

**What you'll notice:** No systematic proactive outreach before renewal. No review request process after policy changes or claims. No cross-sell trigger for clients who bought home coverage but don't have an umbrella policy — common gap in a high-net-worth market like Southlake/Colleyville.

**Opener:** "In an independent agency your size, what does the process look like for reaching out to a client 45 days before their renewal — before the carrier does? I ask because the agencies that stay front-of-mind before renewal are the ones that don't lose clients to a broker who called two weeks before the policy came up."

**Hook:** Renewal outreach + policy review trigger. A Southlake agency with 400 active personal-lines clients that loses 8% annually to renewal shopping is losing 32 clients per year. A 45-day proactive review sequence that cuts that to 5% recovers 12 clients per year at $1,200 average annual premium = $14,400 in retained revenue from one change to the outreach workflow.

**Best time:** 9:30am–11:30am.

## Target 4: CPA or Accounting Firm — Southlake or Westlake

**Search:** "CPA Southlake TX" or "accountant Colleyville TX" — look for small independent firms (2–8 people), not H&R Block, not the big regionals. Firm names with a person's last name or something like "[Name] & Associates."

**What to look for:** Firm doing tax prep plus ongoing bookkeeping or advisory for small business clients. They have a service delivery calendar that runs on trust and manual memory — quarterly close reminders, annual tax document collection, year-end planning conversations. None of it is automated.

**What you'll notice:** Post-tax-season contact drops to near zero. The firm is busy. The client assumes everything is fine. By November, when year-end planning would add real value, the window has narrowed and neither side has initiated it. Referral requests after tax season — when trust is highest — almost never happen.

**Opener:** "I know you just finished tax season. I'm curious about what happens in May and June with your ongoing business clients — do you have a systematic process for year-end planning outreach or annual review scheduling, or does that depend on the client reaching out?" The referral angle: "Most CPAs I work with say their best clients come from referrals, but they don't have a process for asking at the moment when a client is most likely to say yes — which is right after tax season closes and they're feeling good about the outcome."

**Hook:** Year-end planning outreach + referral timing. A CPA with 40 business clients that generates 1 additional referral per quarter from post-tax-season systematic asks adds 4 new business clients per year. At $3,500 average annual engagement, that's $14,000 in new revenue from a process that takes 5 minutes per client.

**Best time:** 10am–noon Tuesday. Still catching up from April 30 filing deadlines, but the worst of it is behind them.

## Target 5: Estate Planning or Family Law Attorney — Southlake

**Search:** "estate planning attorney Southlake TX" or "estate attorney Colleyville TX" — look for small independent firms (1–4 attorneys). Southlake and Westlake have a high concentration of high-net-worth individuals who need estate planning, and many of the families who had plans drafted 10+ years ago are overdue for a review.

**What to look for:** Solo or small estate planning practice. Website that mentions wills, trusts, powers of attorney, probate. Been open 5+ years. Likely has a client base that includes people in or approaching retirement with significant assets — the exact clients who need plan reviews every 3–5 years and aren't being contacted proactively.

**What you'll notice:** No systematic plan-review outreach. An estate plan drafted in 2018 is probably stale — tax laws changed, beneficiaries may have changed, family situations changed. The attorney knows this. The client doesn't know they need a review. Nobody is prompting them.

**Opener:** "For an estate planning practice, what does your process look like for reaching out to clients whose plans are 5 years old? I ask because the window between when a plan is drafted and when it needs updating is usually shorter than clients expect — and most attorneys I talk to don't have an automated way to flag those clients before a family event makes the gap obvious."

**Hook:** Plan review outreach. An attorney with 200 completed estate plans from the past 8 years has 60–80 of them that are overdue for a review. A systematic 5-year review reminder converts 25–35% of those contacts into a review engagement at $800–$2,500 per update. One campaign, 20 responses, $16,000–$50,000 in review work — from clients who already trust the attorney.

**Best time:** 10am–noon or 2–4pm. Attorneys in small practices are often available for a 5-minute walk-in conversation.

## Target 6: Independent Mortgage Broker — Southlake or Colleyville

**Search:** "mortgage broker Southlake TX" — look for independent brokers, not bank loan officers. Independent mortgage brokers in high-income markets like Southlake/Colleyville handle jumbo loans, refinances for high-net-worth clients, and commercial property financing.

**What to look for:** 1–4 person shop, not a bank branch, with a client database of people who closed transactions in the past 3–5 years.

**What you'll notice:** Zero post-close follow-up. Once the transaction closes, the broker moves to the next deal. But every client who closed in 2022 or 2023 at a high rate is a potential refinance conversation if rates drop another 50–75 basis points — and they need to hear from someone they already trust before they hear from a competitor's prospecting campaign.

**Opener:** "How many of your past clients from 2022 or 2023 are still at the rate they closed at? I ask because the brokers who are set up to reach out proactively when rates shift — even a partial shift — are the ones who capture the refinance before their past clients find another lender. Is that something you have a system for, or does it depend on the client calling you?"

**Hook:** Rate alert automation + past client reactivation. A broker with 80 closed transactions from 2022–2023 who can trigger a proactive rate-check outreach when their client's rate becomes 75+ basis points refinanceable has a pipeline of warm refinance conversations that no competitor can poach first.

**Best time:** 9:30–11am or 2–4pm.

## Zone Notes

- **Southlake Town Square area (FM 1709 / Carroll Ave):** Highest density of professional services offices. Street-level walk-ins are easy — small office suites, easy parking in structured lots. Start here.
- **Colleyville (Glade Rd / Bedford Rd):** More suburban strip commercial. Slightly more blue-collar mix than Southlake but still high-income. Good for insurance agencies and CPA firms.
- **Westlake:** Very small town — most businesses are in Southlake or Keller, not technically in the Westlake town limits. Don't bother driving specifically to Westlake — the market is served from Southlake and Keller approaches.
- **Drive time from Dallas:** Southlake is 30–35 minutes northwest on SH 114 or DFW area roads. Leave by 9:15am.
- **Tone for this zone:** Softer sell than Garland/Mesquite. These are professional service owners who've heard plenty of pitches. Don't open with "here's what your problem is." Open with a question about their process, let them identify the gap, then show them the math. They respond to numbers, not urgency.
