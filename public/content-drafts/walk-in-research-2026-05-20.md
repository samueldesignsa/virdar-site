# Walk-In Research — Wednesday, May 20, 2026

**Zone: Carrollton / Farmers Branch, TX — I-35E Corridor + Old Denton Rd / Marsh Lane + Belt Line Rd**

Why Carrollton/Farmers Branch today: The recent rotation has covered north (Frisco, McKinney, Plano, Richardson, Addison) and west (Irving/Las Colinas). Carrollton and Farmers Branch are northwest — different direction, different business character. The I-35E corridor through Carrollton is dense with independent professional services, auto shops, and light industry. Farmers Branch has a commercial cluster near the LBJ/35E interchange with insurance agencies, property management firms, and professional services that have been there 15–20 years. Carrollton also has a large Asian-American business community (particularly along Old Denton Rd and Marsh Lane) — Chinese-American and Vietnamese-American business owners who are entrepreneurial, underserved by most tech vendors, and often bilingual. Wednesday is a solid midweek day for professional services and shop owners alike.

## Target 1: Independent CPA / Accounting Firm — Carrollton Professional Corridors

**What they do:** Tax preparation, bookkeeping, small business accounting. Independent firm (1–4 CPAs), not a national chain. Many serve the local Asian-American business community — restaurants, nail salons, retail shops, small contractors.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "CPA Carrollton TX" or "accounting firm Carrollton TX." Check Old Denton Rd, Marsh Lane, and the Josey Lane / Keller Springs area for small professional office suites. Look for local or founder-name branding, open 5+ years, fewer than 30 Google reviews (underdigitized is good).

**What to notice:** Post-tax season (April 15) lull. These firms are heads-down January through April 15 and almost entirely client-driven. May is quiet. That means the owner has time to have a conversation you couldn't have gotten in March. More importantly: most small CPA firms have zero off-season client communication. Clients who got a good return in April haven't heard from their accountant since. No mid-year check-in, no tax planning reminder, no estimated payment notification. The client who drifts to a competitor does it between April and October.

The bilingual opportunity: If the firm serves Vietnamese-American or Chinese-American business owners, ask whether their outreach goes out in the client's language. Most firms rely on phone calls; a client who prefers written communication in their native language is getting a worse experience.

**Hook:** "I work with accounting firms on one specific gap — what happens between April 16 and January 1. Most of the firms I talk to have great client relationships during tax season and zero proactive contact the rest of the year. I'm curious: outside of their next appointment, when does your average client hear from you?"

**Revenue math:** A 120-client CPA firm retaining 8 additional clients per year who would have otherwise drifted saves $960–$2,400 per retained client (average annual fee $120–$300). That's $7,680–$19,200 in preserved revenue from an automated off-season sequence.

## Target 2: Independent Dental Practice — Old Denton Rd / Marsh Lane / Josey Lane Corridors

**What they do:** General or family dentistry. Solo or two-doctor practice, not a DSO or Aspen Dental. Look for local branding, 4+ years in business, 60–200 Google reviews.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "dentist Carrollton TX" or "family dentist Carrollton TX." The Marsh Lane and Old Denton Rd corridors have strip center medical suites with independent practices. Also check the Josey Lane area near Keller Springs. Carrollton has above-average dental practice density relative to DFW — it's a good hunting ground.

**What to notice:** Carrollton dental practices serving the Asian-American community often have Chinese- or Vietnamese-speaking staff already. If the practice has bilingual capability at the front desk but doesn't send bilingual recall messages, that's a specific gap to raise. The recall problem is the same as everywhere: patients due for a cleaning get one reminder, don't respond, and age off the active list. But in a practice with multilingual patients, the recall effectiveness may be lower than the English-only baseline because the outreach doesn't match the patient's preferred communication style.

Check Google reviews: how many mention the doctor's name by first name? That's a relationship-driven practice — the owner will respond to the relationship angle, not the efficiency angle.

**Hook:** "Dental practices consistently have the same gap: the patient who was due for a cleaning three months ago and didn't respond to the first reminder. I'm curious — for that specific group, what does your second and third touch look like? And if the patient prefers to be reached in another language, does your system handle that?"

**Revenue math:** A practice with 800 active patients improving annual return rate from 74% to 83% adds 72 hygiene visits. At $185/visit, that's $13,320 in direct recall revenue — plus restorative treatment booked at those visits. Bilingual outreach in a Carrollton practice can move recall rates meaningfully in a market where English-only reminders don't land.

## Target 3: Auto Repair Shop — I-35E Service Roads / Frankford Rd / Whitlock Ln

**What they do:** Independent general auto repair — brakes, diagnostics, tires, oil changes, scheduled maintenance. Owner-operator. Not a national chain.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "auto repair Carrollton TX" or "auto shop Frankford Carrollton." The I-35E service roads between Trinity Mills Rd and Frankford Rd have a cluster of independent shops. Also check Whitlock Lane and the light industrial pocket east of I-35E near Keller Springs. Look for shops with 80–250 reviews, open 5+ years.

**What to notice:** Two gaps — declined service follow-up and maintenance interval outreach — are universal, but the Carrollton shop market has a third: the Asian-American customer base. Vietnamese-owned shops (they exist in this corridor) and shops serving Vietnamese or Chinese customers often have customers who prefer text communication over phone calls. If the shop is calling back customers who prefer texts, the response rate on declined service follow-up is low. A text-based follow-up system that matches customer communication preference isn't a feature — it's table stakes for a shop in this market.

**Hook:** "Auto shops I work with have two follow-up gaps: declined services that never get revisited, and maintenance intervals that pass without the shop reaching out. In a shop serving a mix of customers — English-speaking and otherwise — I'm also curious whether your follow-up goes out the way your customers actually communicate. A phone call to someone who prefers text is the same as not calling."

**Revenue math:** A shop doing 50 declined service write-ups monthly at $370 average, converting 18% through systematic follow-up, recovers $39,960 per year in diagnosed work that walked out the door.

## Target 5: Property Management Company — Farmers Branch / Carrollton

**What they do:** Property management for single-family rentals, small apartment complexes, or mixed portfolios. Independent company (1–5 employees), not a national franchise.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "property management Farmers Branch TX" or "property management Carrollton TX." Farmers Branch has significant rental housing stock from its suburban build-out years — a lot of it managed by small independent PM companies. Look for firms with 20–80 reviews, in business 5+ years, and not affiliated with a major franchise (no FirstService, Greystar).

**What to notice:** Three automation gaps in small PM companies: (1) maintenance request routing — tenant submits a request and it sits in an inbox until someone manually dispatches a vendor; (2) lease renewal outreach — residents don't get contacted until 30–60 days out, when it's already late; (3) delinquency communication — the late rent follow-up is a phone call no one wants to make. Any of these is a real conversation. The lease renewal gap is the most financially acute — a vacancy costs $2,000–$4,000 in lost rent and turnover, so retaining a good tenant with systematic 90-day advance outreach pays for itself immediately.

**Hook:** "Property management companies I work with consistently have the same three gaps: maintenance requests that get routed manually, lease renewals that don't start the conversation early enough, and late rent follow-up that depends on whoever has bandwidth. I'm curious which of those is most painful for you right now."

**Revenue math:** A PM company with 80 units reducing vacancy duration from 21 days to 14 days through earlier renewal outreach eliminates 5.6 vacancy-days per unit per year. At $1,600/month average rent, that's $8,960 in prevented lost rent per year for that portfolio.

## Target 6: Roofing Contractor — Carrollton / I-35E Area

**What they do:** Storm damage roofing — insurance claims work, repair, full replacement. Independent roofing company (5–25 employees), not a major chain.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "roofing contractor Carrollton TX" or "roofing company Farmers Branch TX." Also look for trucks in the industrial pockets along I-35E. Look for local branding, 50–300 reviews, open 5+ years.

**What to notice:** Hail season follow-up window. DFW's March–May hail events generated estimate surges for roofing contractors. A contractor who wrote 40–60 estimates in March and April has likely followed up on fewer than 20 of them. Homeowners who got an estimate and didn't call back aren't always choosing a competitor — many are just waiting and haven't had someone call them in three weeks. The estimate follow-up gap is the most tangible revenue problem a roofing contractor has in May.

Also check their Google reviews: are they responding? A contractor with 80 reviews and 6 responses has a visible gap that a walk-in can turn into an immediate conversation point.

**Hook:** "Roofing contractors I talk to in this area wrote a lot of estimates during the March storms. I'm curious — for the estimates where the homeowner hasn't called back, does your company have a systematic follow-up process, or does it depend on whether a salesperson has time to call? Because the ones who don't hear from you in the next 10 days are often choosing whoever follows up first, not whoever gave the best estimate."

**Revenue math:** A contractor with 40 outstanding estimates at $14,000 average job value, converting 3 additional jobs through systematic follow-up, adds $42,000 in revenue from work already scoped.

**Best time:** 8–10am or after 1pm. Roofing contractor offices are often active early before crews go out or after noon when they're back from site visits. Avoid 10am–noon if the owner is doing estimates.
