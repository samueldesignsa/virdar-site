## Zone Notes

**Bishop Arts is a tight-knit business community.** These owners know each other. A good conversation with the salon owner on Bishop Ave may produce a referral to the dental practice on Davis St two blocks away. The neighborhood identity is strong — business owners are proud of the independent, local character of the district. Beverly should open with that: "I work with independent businesses" lands better here than "I work with small businesses." The word "independent" carries weight in Bishop Arts.

**Revenue math is smaller than Park Cities but response rate is higher.** A Bishop Arts chiro practice or salon is not running $110,000 seasonal campaigns. The numbers are $3,000 to $8,000 per engagement. But these owners are less defended, more curious, and more likely to have a 20-minute conversation. Beverly can move through 4-5 businesses in a morning without hitting skeptical gatekeepers.

**The July 4 hook is the most timely offer.** 16 days out. Dental, fitness, and vet all have July 4 leave-behinds now. Use the specific day-count: "July 4 is 16 days away" is a time anchor that gets attention better than any abstract pitch about automation.

**After Bishop Arts: Kessler Park toward Fort Worth Ave.** The commercial strip on Fort Worth Ave (between Jefferson and Sylvan) has a cluster of independent practices — a vet clinic, a dental office, and a few personal care businesses. Beverly can walk that on the way back to the car.
