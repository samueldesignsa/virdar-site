## Target 1: Boutique Fitness Studio — Lower Greenville or Henderson Avenue

**What they do:** Independent boutique fitness studio (Pilates, yoga, HIIT, cycling, or barre) serving the Lakewood and Lower Greenville neighborhood. These studios have 150–300 members paying $100–$145/month on recurring memberships. July 4 is 17 days away — the highest member lapse week of the year for boutique fitness. The studios that manage this are reaching unbooked members and paused memberships right now. The ones that don't are managing cancellations in August.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "boutique fitness Lower Greenville Dallas," "Pilates studio Henderson Ave Dallas," "yoga studio Lakewood Dallas." Independent owner-operated studio — not an Orangetheory franchise, not a CorePower corporate location. Owner-operator visible on the website or Instagram. 50–300 reviews.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- Class booking calendar: Is July 4 week visible? Are classes already running low or are they barely booked? A sparse July 4 calendar is the opening hook.
- Instagram or email: Any pre-holiday messaging visible in recent posts? Almost never. That's the gap.
- Membership pause policy: Is there a freeze option visible on the website? Studios with easy freeze options tend to have more pre-holiday freezes — more members in the at-risk category.

**Hook (new blog post live today):**
"July 4 is 17 days away. Your members who are traveling are going to cancel their reservations in the next week. The members who stay home are going to take a break because of the holiday and not come back until late July or August. In a 200-member studio, that's 40 to 50 members at risk of a lapse pattern — and the ones who lapse for more than three weeks cancel at a 40 percent rate. The studios that protect their July retention send one message this week to members who haven't booked July 4 week classes yet: a specific date, a specific class, one-tap booking. That message keeps 25 to 30 members in their routine through the holiday. At your membership rate, that's $3,000 to $4,000 in retained monthly revenue plus the downstream renewals those members would have canceled. What does your pre-holiday retention outreach look like right now?"

**Revenue math:**
- 220-member studio at $130/month; 25% lapse rate over holiday = 55 members at risk
- 60% retention with proactive outreach = 33 retained × $130/month × 7 months average = $30,030 in retained revenue
- Frozen membership reactivation: 18 frozen members, 60% restart with outreach = 11 retained × $130 × 6 months = $8,580

## Target 3: Chiropractic or Physical Therapy Practice — Lakewood or East Dallas

**What they do:** Independent chiropractic or physical therapy practice serving Lakewood and East Dallas residents. These practices have a specific summer revenue problem: patients who finish active care in the spring with a standing "come in when it flares up" instruction and don't hear from the practice again. Combined with unused insurance benefit — many employer plans renew January 1, and patients who didn't use chiro benefits in the first half of the year have unused coverage through December.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "chiropractor Lakewood Dallas," "physical therapy East Dallas," "chiropractic Gaston Avenue." Independent practice. Not a chain. Owner-practitioner or small 2-doctor group. 50–200 reviews.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- Mid-year benefit outreach: Does the website mention using chiro benefits before year-end? Almost never.

A message to those patients now — 'you have coverage remaining through year-end, here are maintenance appointments through summer we can reserve' — gets a 45 to 55 percent response rate. For a practice your size, that's 20 to 30 patients returning for 2 to 3 visits each before December. What does your past-patient reactivation look like?"

**Revenue math:**
- 80 patients who finished active care in Q1; 50% respond to mid-year benefit outreach = 40 returning
