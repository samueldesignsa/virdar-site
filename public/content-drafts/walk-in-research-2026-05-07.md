### Why this zone today

Southlake and Colleyville are two of the highest-income zip codes in the DFW Metroplex. Median household income in Southlake (76092) runs north of $225,000. These are communities where clients have meaningful assets — real homes, real vehicles, real business exposure — and where the difference between adequate coverage and excellent coverage matters financially. The independent agency is also directly threatened in this market. State Farm, Allstate, and Farmers all have heavy presences in Southlake and Grapevine. An independent agency competing here does so on relationship and service — and the gap between what they promise on relationship and what they actually deliver in systematic follow-up is exactly where automation helps most.

**Find it:** Search "Higginbotham insurance Southlake TX" or "Higginbotham insurance Grapevine TX" on Google Maps. Higginbotham is a large Texas-based independent agency with multiple North Texas offices.

**What they do:** Full-service independent P&C agency, commercial and personal lines. Large firm by independent agency standards — multiple producers, dedicated account managers, established book of business in the affluent suburban markets.

**What I noticed:** Higginbotham's size is a double-edged sword. They have the client base and premium volume to benefit significantly from automation (a 4% retention improvement on a large book is substantial). But larger agencies often have more entrenched processes and may have already evaluated off-the-shelf solutions that didn't fit. The automation pitch here is customization — what AgencyBloc or Applied Epic's built-in automation can't do is what Virdar builds.

**Hook:** "Larger books mean more renewals hitting every week — does your team have bandwidth to run a full renewal sequence for every personal lines client the way you would for your top commercial accounts?" If the answer is "we try, but it's hard to be consistent across the full book," that's the opening. The automation case is about consistency at scale.

**Best approach:** Ask for the operations manager or the marketing director rather than a producer. This is a process conversation, not an individual producer conversation.

**What I noticed:** Mid-Cities independent agencies are a strong target because they sit at the intersection of high-value residential clients (Colleyville) and a commercial market (Bedford/Euless industrial and restaurant corridor) that has active cross-sell potential. The claims follow-up story lands particularly well here: a client who has a commercial claim navigated without personal outreach from their agent is a client who is actively shopping at renewal.

"What if your system automatically sent a check-in at day 7 and day 30 of every open claim — from you, not from the carrier? What would that do to retention on those accounts?"

## Target 4: Grapevine-Based Independent Agency (Near Main Street / Historic District)

**Find it:** Search "insurance agency Grapevine TX" on Google Maps. Grapevine's historic downtown area has a mix of professional services, and several insurance agencies operate in the Main Street corridor.

**What they do:** Typically boutique independent agencies, strong personal lines focus, some commercial. Owner-operated with 1 to 3 producers. Small book: $400K to $1.2M in written premium. These are the agencies where the owner is also the lead producer.

**What I noticed:** Owner-operated agencies in Grapevine are a particularly good walk-in target because the owner is the one who feels every lost client personally. They don't have a VP of Sales to pass the conversation to. When you describe a client who switches to a competitor because of a missed renewal follow-up, they know exactly what that experience feels like — they've lived it. The emotional resonance of the pitch is immediate.

**Hook:** "How many clients do you think you've lost in the last two years where the relationship was fine, but they just didn't hear from you at the right moment?" Let them answer. If they say "probably a few," walk through the renewal sequence math: at 10% annual attrition on a $600K book, that's $60,000 in premium leaving every year. If automation catches one in five of those — holds 2 percent instead of losing 10 percent — the math is $12,000 in retained premium, $1,440 in retained commission. The system pays for itself in year one.

**Best approach:** This is a direct owner-to-owner conversation. Don't ask for anyone — the owner is almost certainly at the desk. "I work with independent agencies on their client follow-up systems — do you have five minutes?"

## Target 5: Commercial Lines Specialist — Trophy Club or Westlake Area

**Find it:** Search "commercial insurance agency Trophy Club TX" or "business insurance Westlake TX" on Google Maps. Trophy Club and Westlake have a concentration of professional services firms and small businesses that generate commercial insurance demand.

**What they do:** Independent agencies that have built a commercial book alongside or instead of personal lines. Commercial accounts generate higher premiums and higher commissions per policy — a single commercial account might be worth $5,000 to $25,000 in annual premium.

**What I noticed:** Commercial accounts have more complex renewal needs (certificates of insurance, premium audits, endorsements) that generate ongoing client contact — but the relationship management between renewals is often thin. A commercial client who goes 10 months without hearing from their agent is a client who is receptive to a competitor offering a "mid-year coverage review." The annual review automation story is specifically strong for commercial lines.

**Hook:** "For your commercial accounts, what does the relationship look like between renewals? Outside of a certificate request or a policy change, are you proactively reaching out?" If the answer is "mostly just at renewal," the annual review scheduling sequence is the specific opportunity. "What if every commercial client got a mid-year touchpoint — a 20-minute call scheduled automatically 6 months after renewal — to check on coverage needs before the renewal conversation gets hard?"
