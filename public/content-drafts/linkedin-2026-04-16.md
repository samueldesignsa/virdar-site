Most home service businesses send an estimate and then give up after one unanswered call.

The customer still has the problem. They're still going to hire someone. They just haven't decided who yet.

The businesses that follow up consistently — not once, but at day 2, day 5, and day 10 — close 30% to 40% of those open estimates. The ones that call once and move on close about 10%.

That's not a sales skill gap. It's a systems gap.

An automated follow-up sequence running on every open estimate doesn't require your team to remember anything. It sends the message, logs the response, and flags the warm ones for a real conversation.

If you have 20 open estimates right now and you close 4 more because of consistent follow-up, at a $400 average ticket, that's $1,600 from work you already did.

The estimate was already the hard part. Don't leave the close on the table because of one unanswered call.
