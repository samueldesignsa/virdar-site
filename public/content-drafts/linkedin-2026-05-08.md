# LinkedIn Post — Friday, May 8, 2026

**Topic:** Referral timing — the 48-hour window after a positive outcome. Ask at the peak moment vs. 90 days later = 3–5x conversion difference.

**Post for today (Friday). Best window: 7–9am CT or 11am–1pm CT.**

---

A customer picks up their car after a $3,200 engine repair. Everything went smoothly. The tech communicated throughout. The car runs perfectly.

That customer would refer three people to you right now, today, if you asked.

Ask them at their next oil change — 90 days from now — and the same enthusiasm is background noise. They've moved on. The moment has passed.

This applies everywhere: the patient who got a diagnosis they were worried about and it turned out fine. The homeowner whose HVAC got fixed before the first cold front hit. The client who just closed on their house after a stressful process.

Peak referral window: 24–48 hours after a positive outcome. Most businesses miss it entirely — not because they don't want referrals, but because no one remembered to ask while the feeling was still there.

An automated post-visit sequence closes this gap. A text at 24 hours: "How did everything go? We'd love to hear." If they respond positively, the follow-up sends: "We really appreciate that — if you know anyone who needs [service], we'd love to help them."

The referral was already there. The timing is what determines whether it happens.
