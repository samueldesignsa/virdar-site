A customer leaves a 5-star review. The business doesn't respond.

Two weeks later, another one. Silence.

The business has 43 reviews. They've responded to 6 of them.

Here's why this matters: 89% of consumers read responses to reviews before deciding whether to contact a business. A pattern of no responses signals that nobody's minding the store. The reviews are there. The trust isn't.

Responding to reviews also affects local search ranking. Google's algorithm factors in review activity — both volume and recency of responses — when deciding which businesses show up in the top three local results.

The fix is not complicated. An automated system flags every new review, drafts a response based on the review content, and posts it (or queues it for approval) within hours. You're not ignoring your customers. You're not paying someone to watch Google My Business all day. It just runs.

The businesses that respond to every review — within a day, every time — will outrank the ones that don't. That gap is already building in most markets.
