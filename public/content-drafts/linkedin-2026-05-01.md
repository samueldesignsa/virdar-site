**Topic:** The post-visit follow-up gap — the 24-hour window most businesses ignore

---

After a customer finishes a service, most businesses go completely silent.

No check-in. No care instructions. No confirmation that the thing worked. Just — nothing.

For appointment-based businesses, that 24-hour window after a visit is the highest-value communication window you have. The client is thinking about you. The experience is fresh. If something went wrong, they're going to tell someone — the question is whether it's you or Yelp.

A simple automated follow-up, sent the next morning, does three things at once:
1. Catches any problems before they become bad reviews
2. Shows the client the practice cares about the outcome, not just the transaction
3. Creates the natural moment to ask for a Google review — when the experience is still top of mind

The businesses that ask for reviews immediately after a positive outcome get reviews. The ones that wait until the next visit, or send a mass request three weeks later, get ignored.

The post-visit window is 24 hours. After that, the moment is gone.

Does your follow-up run automatically, or does it depend on someone remembering?
