### 1. Blog Post #30 — Cleaning Services and Maid Services
**File:** `public/blog/ai-automation-for-cleaning-services.html`
**Live at:** virdar.co/blog/ai-automation-for-cleaning-services.html

Cleaning services is the most underleveraged industry in the Virdar content library — high recurring revenue, predictable churn patterns, and walk-in targets all over DFW. No post existed for it.

June is the right moment for this post. It's peak move season in North Texas (May–August), which means:
1. Move-in and move-out cleans are at their highest volume
2. New-to-neighborhood families are the most likely to start recurring service
3. Spring pauses (tax season budget cuts, houses that were on the market) are exactly 60–90 days old — right in the recovery window

The post covers five revenue gaps:

1. **Lapsed client reactivation (the "on pause" problem)** — The window to recover paused accounts is 30–60 days. After 90 days, the client has either adjusted to cleaning it themselves or found another service. A message at week 5 — "whenever you're ready, your slot is here" — recovers a significant portion. Opening story: DeSoto cleaning company loses three paused clients who all intended to return but found something easier to book when they were ready.

2. **Last-minute cancellation recovery** — A same-day rescheduling offer with two specific time options rebounds 35–40% of morning-of cancellations. Most companies absorb these as lost revenue. The offer doesn't need to be a hard sell — it's a practical message that makes rebooking easier than the client expected.

3. **Move-in clean to recurring service conversion** — The 72-hour follow-up after a move-in clean, with a specific slot offer (not a generic "let us know if you're interested"), converts at 35% versus 12% for a passive follow-up. June and July are peak move season in DFW — this is the highest-leverage conversion window of the year.

4. **Referral asks from satisfied clients** — Cleaning is a neighborhood referral industry. The satisfied client mentions their cleaner constantly in passing. What they don't have is a ready mechanism to connect that conversation to an actual booking. A referral link in a post-clean message closes that gap.

5. **Review generation** — 24-hour post-clean review request. Moving from 38 reviews at 4.6 to 90+ reviews at 4.8 dramatically changes local search visibility in a "cleaning service [city]" search.

Revenue math in the post:
- Lapsed reactivation: 35 paused accounts at 38% response = 13 recovered clients × $175/month = $2,275/month in recurring revenue
- Cancellation recovery: 8 cancellations/month at 38% rebook = $525/month recovered
- Move-in conversion: 6 move-in cleans/month at 35% recurring conversion, 14-month average retention = $4,778/month in added lifetime value
