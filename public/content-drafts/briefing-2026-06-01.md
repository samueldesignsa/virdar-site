### 1. Blog Post #29 — Painting Contractors
**File:** `public/blog/ai-automation-for-painting-contractors.html`
**Live at:** virdar.co/blog/ai-automation-for-painting-contractors.html

Today is June 1. First day of peak exterior painting season in DFW. Painting contractors were the obvious industry gap — every major trade (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, pest control) has a post, but painting didn't. The industry has the same core problems: estimates that go dark, past customers nobody reactivates, visible jobs that never produce a review or a neighborhood referral.

The post covers five gaps:
1. **Estimate follow-up before the season fills** — The homeowner who said "call me in May" in April and never got a second message. Most didn't say no; they said not yet. A 48-hour + 14-day follow-up sequence is the difference between a 32% and 47% close rate.
2. **Seasonal reactivation of past customers** — Exterior paint is a 7–10 year cycle. A painter operating 3–5 years in Mansfield has 80+ past exterior customers whose first repaint is coming up right now. Nobody is contacting them.
3. **Neighborhood referral asks after visible jobs** — Exterior painting is more visible than almost any other service job. The satisfied homeowner will recommend the painter — if they're asked at 72 hours. Most painters never ask.
4. **Upsell outreach in the 60-day window** — Decks, fences, interior rooms. The window is open for 60 days after an exterior job. A 30-day follow-up converts at 22%.
5. **Review generation after every completed job** — Painting is review-driven at local search. The painter with 31 reviews at 4.5 is invisible behind the one with 110 reviews at 4.8. Nobody asks, so nobody leaves them.

Revenue math in the post:
- Estimate follow-up: 20 estimates/month at 32% → 47% close = 3 additional jobs × $3,800 = $68,400/exterior season
- Seasonal reactivation: 300 past customers, 80 at the 4–5 year mark at 18% response = 14 conversations → 6 booked jobs × $3,200 = $19,200
- Neighborhood referrals: 10 exterior jobs/month at 12% referral response = 7 additional jobs/season × $3,800 = $26,600

## One Insight

June is when the feast-or-famine cycle hits hardest for seasonal contractors — painters, landscapers, pool service, HVAC. May was busy. The pipeline is full through mid-June. It feels like there's no need to worry about new business.

The contractors who will have a strong August are the ones doing pipeline work in June, not the ones who figure it out when the backlog clears. The estimate follow-up, the past-customer reactivation campaign, the neighborhood referral ask after today's finished jobs — none of that produces revenue today. It produces revenue in 6 to 8 weeks.

For Beverly's walk-ins: the hook that works best with seasonal contractors in their busy period isn't "you're leaving money on the table right now." It's "August is 8 weeks away. What does your pipeline look like for the second half of the season?" That question puts the urgency in a place they can feel it — they've lived through a slow August before.
