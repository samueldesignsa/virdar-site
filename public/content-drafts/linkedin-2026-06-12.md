# LinkedIn Post — Friday, June 12, 2026

A chiropractor in Richardson told me he has 80 patients who finished their treatment plans in Q1.

None of them have heard from him since.

Half of them have unused chiropractic benefit left on their insurance — $600 to $800 sitting there through December. The patient who finished their plan in March and whose back is now "doing fine" doesn't know that. They're not calling. They're waiting until October when the pain is bad enough again and they've forgotten they still had coverage.

A message in June — "you have remaining chiropractic benefit through the end of the year, and we have open mornings in July" — brings back 30 to 40 percent of those patients before the benefit lapses and before they're in pain.

That's not a marketing campaign. That's a timing conversation with patients who are already in the system, already covered, and already coming back eventually. You're just deciding whether they come back in July or October.

Mid-year is the inflection point. Half the coverage year is over. The practices that mention it in June keep the appointment. The ones that don't wait for the pain.
