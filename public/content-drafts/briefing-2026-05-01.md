## One Insight

Today is the best possible day to walk into a CPA or bookkeeper, and it's worth prioritizing higher than it looks on a Friday.

May 1 is the first business day after April 30. For most small CPA firms, April 30 is the unofficial end of the extension-request crunch — the stretch from March 15 through April 30 when owners work 60-hour weeks and everything non-urgent gets ignored. Today, they've surfaced. They're catching up on email. They're thinking about what Q2 looks like.

The pitch isn't "let me automate your tax prep." CPAs are protective of their core work. The pitch is: "You have 50 ongoing business clients who trust you more than anyone else in their financial life. Do you have a systematic way to schedule annual reviews, send referral requests, or stay in contact between tax seasons? Most firms I talk to do it manually — or not at all."

The trust CPAs have with small business clients is the highest of any service provider. A CPA firm that automates its client touchpoint calendar — quarterly check-ins, annual review scheduling, referral asks after tax season — has a retention and referral machine that most firms would pay significant money for. And right now, today, the CPA is sitting at their desk with coffee, a cleared inbox, and mental space to have a real conversation.

Don't skip that office.
