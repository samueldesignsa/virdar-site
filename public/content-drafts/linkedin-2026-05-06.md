# LinkedIn Post — Wednesday, May 6, 2026

**Topic:** Speed-to-lead — the 5-minute response window. A form submission or after-hours call that doesn't get a response within 5 minutes is 21x less likely to convert than one that does. Most small businesses respond in 6+ hours.

**Post for today (Wednesday). Best window: 7–9am CT or 11am–1pm CT.**

---

A lead fills out a contact form on your website at 7:48pm on a Tuesday.

You see it Wednesday morning at 8:30am. You call back at 9am.

That's a 13-hour gap. The research on this is pretty clear: a lead contacted within 5 minutes is 21 times more likely to convert than one contacted after 30 minutes. At 13 hours, you're not in the running.

The lead didn't go dark because they changed their mind. They called the next two businesses on the list. One of those picked up or responded within minutes. That's who they booked with.

This applies to every service business: dental practices, financial advisors, law firms, home service companies. The businesses that win on lead conversion aren't necessarily the best at their craft. They're the fastest to respond.

Automated lead response fires a text within 90 seconds of form submission, regardless of the time of day. "Hey, this is [Business] — I just saw your message. Happy to answer any questions now or get you on the schedule. What works for you?" Simple. Personal. Immediate.

The call back still happens. The human relationship still gets built. The automation just makes sure you're in the conversation before the lead moves on.
