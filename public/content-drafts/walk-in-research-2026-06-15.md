Southlake practices often have strong review volume because the patient base is digitally active.

**What to notice before walking in:**
- July 4 week appointments: Any practice with 80+ appointments scheduled June 29 to July 3 is sitting on a 20–24% cancellation exposure. Beverly can open with "you're two weeks out from July 4 week — what does your cancellation rate look like that week compared to a normal week?" - Back-to-school messaging: Anything on the website or Google profile about August scheduling? Almost never visible. Beverly has found this gap in every DFW market.

**Hook (two options — use based on what you see in the first 30 seconds):**

*Option A — July 4 week (most timely today):*
"You're two weeks out from July 4 week. In a practice your size, you're probably looking at 16 to 22 last-minute cancellations that week — families finalizing vacation plans in the next five to seven days and cancelling the Tuesday before they leave. The practices that protect that week send a message this week: 'your appointment is confirmed, if your plans change we have openings July 15–16.' That message drops holiday cancellations by 50 to 60 percent. What does your July 4 week cancellation rate look like typically?"

*Option B — Back-to-school recall (if July 4 hook doesn't land):*
"Carroll ISD starts mid-August. You have families right now — 80 to 120 kids in your system — who are planning to get a cleaning done before school. They haven't called. They'll call in August when your July morning slots are already booked. A message to your overdue pediatric patients this week fills those slots from your own list. What does your proactive recall look like for pediatric patients this summer?"

**Revenue math (July 4 angle):** 85 appointments booked for June 30–July 3; 22% unmanaged cancellation rate vs. 10% with proactive outreach; 10 recovered appointments at $225 average = $2,250 recovered in one week. Holiday-week management across all six annual holidays = $22,000–$35,000 per year per provider.

**Revenue math (back-to-school angle):** 750 active patients, 110 overdue pediatric patients; 67% proactive response vs. 36% passive = 34 additional pediatric appointments at $225 average = $7,650.
