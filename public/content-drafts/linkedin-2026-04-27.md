The average no-show rate for appointment-based businesses is 10 to 20 percent.

For a med spa doing 30 appointments a week at $400 average ticket, that's $12,000 to $24,000 in lost revenue every month. Not lost to competition. Not lost to price. Lost to someone who forgot, got busy, and didn't see a reminder that was compelling enough to stop them from letting it slip.

The fix isn't a better cancellation policy. It's a confirmation sequence that treats the appointment like something worth showing up for.

A two-step system: an automated confirmation 48 hours out that requires a response ("reply YES to confirm, or call us to reschedule"), followed by a same-day reminder 2 to 3 hours before the appointment. Practices that run this see no-show rates drop from 15% to under 5% within 60 days.

The response confirmation does two things: it confirms the appointment, and it surfaces the cancellations early enough to fill the slot from a waitlist. A slot that cancels 48 hours out can almost always be filled. A slot that no-shows at noon on Tuesday cannot.

The revenue is already booked. The reminder is the only thing between you and collecting it.
