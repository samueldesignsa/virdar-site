# Walk-In Research — Friday, May 1, 2026

**Zone: Allen / McKinney — US-75 Corridor (75013 / 75070 / 75071)**

New zone. Not previously covered. Allen and McKinney are the fastest-growing Collin County suburbs — dense with independently owned service businesses that serve high-income households. Friday mornings are good: decision-makers are in, less frantic than Monday/Tuesday.

## Target 1: Independent Veterinary Clinic — Allen

**Search:** "veterinary clinic Allen TX" — filter for independently branded practices (not Banfield, VCA, or NVA corporate chains)

**What to look for:** 50–200 Google reviews, "locally owned" language on their website or signage, practice named after the vet or a street (not a corporate brand).

**What you'll likely notice:** No online booking on the website, or booking is a third-party portal they don't fully use. Review count is low relative to how long they've been open.

**Opener:** "I work with independent vet clinics on automation — specifically the annual wellness recall. If a dog came in 11 months ago and hasn't booked their annual yet, does your practice have a system that catches that automatically, or does it depend on your front desk?"

**Hook:** Annual wellness recall. The revenue math is in today's blog post — $7,500 to $10,000/month in appointments that already should have happened.

**Best time:** 9:30–11am Friday. Mornings before the afternoon sick-pet rush.

## Target 2: Independent Veterinary Clinic — McKinney

**Search:** "veterinary clinic McKinney TX" or "animal hospital McKinney TX" — look for independent practices near downtown McKinney or the US-380 corridor.

**What to look for:** Google Maps rating of 4.4–4.8 with 80–180 reviews. Healthy enough practice that they can invest, not so overwhelmed they can't have a conversation.

**Opener:** Same as above, or lead with the no-show angle: "What does a no-show cost you on a dental cleaning day? I ask because most clinics I talk to haven't done the math — the number is usually surprising."

**Hook:** No-show reduction + post-surgery follow-up. A dental cleaning no-show at a vet clinic blocks equipment and anesthesia capacity for half a day.

## Target 3: Pediatric or Family Dental Practice — Allen

**Search:** "pediatric dentist Allen TX" or "family dentist Allen TX" — look for independently branded offices, not Aspen Dental or Kool Smiles chains.

**What to look for:** Website with no online booking, or a booking form that goes to email. Review response rate is poor (can check Google quickly — if they have 150 reviews and the last 20 have no owner response, that's a signal).

**Opener:** "I'm working with a few dental practices on 6-month recall automation — the gap between when a patient should come back and when they actually do. What does your recall process look like right now?"

**Hook:** 6-month hygiene recall. The dental practices I've seen data on are missing 25–35% of their hygiene recall patients in any given 60-day window. At $200–$350 per hygiene visit, that's substantial.

**Best time:** 10am–noon. Before the lunch rush, after the morning appointments are settled.

## Target 4: Boutique Fitness Studio — McKinney

**Search:** "yoga studio McKinney TX" or "pilates McKinney TX" — look for independently owned studios, not franchise locations (not Club Pilates or Pure Barre corporate).

**What to look for:** Class packs or memberships as their pricing model. This matters because it means they have data on who bought a 10-class pack and stopped at 4 — which is a lapsed client reactivation problem.

**Opener:** "Do you track how many people buy a class pack and stop coming before they finish it? I ask because that drop-off point is usually where the biggest retention opportunity is."

**Hook:** Class pack utilization / lapsed member reactivation. A studio selling 10-class packs with 30% of purchasers stopping at 4 is leaving 180 classes on the table per 100 clients. The reactivation message that pulls them back in is automated.

**Best time:** 9:30–10:30am between morning classes, or 12:30–1:30pm.

## Target 5: Independent Chiropractic Practice — Allen

**Search:** "chiropractor Allen TX" — look for independently branded practices (not The Joint franchise locations). The Joint is fully commoditized and has no automation gap to fill.

**What to look for:** Practice that offers care plans or wellness memberships, not just walk-in adjustments. Care plan practices have a compliance problem that's worth solving.

**Opener:** "What percentage of your maintenance patients are actually coming in on the schedule you set for them? I work with chiro practices on the gap between the care plan and actual visit frequency."

**Hook:** Maintenance plan compliance. Today's blog post on PT/chiro (still live: `virdar.co/blog/ai-automation-for-physical-therapy-chiropractic.html`) supports this pitch.

**Best time:** 9–11am. Chiro practices are often quieter Friday mornings — most adjustment-heavy days are Mon/Wed/Fri, so owners are in but not running behind yet.

## Target 6: Independent Optometry Practice — McKinney

**Search:** "optometrist McKinney TX" — look for independently owned practices (not LensCrafters or Walmart Vision).

**What to look for:** Annual exam recall gap. Patients get glasses or contacts and don't come back for 18–24 months. The practice has their prescription and their email address and sends nothing.

**Opener:** "Does your practice have an automated system for annual exam reminders? I'm curious how you handle patients who haven't been back in 13 or 14 months — that's usually the drop-off window."

**Hook:** Annual exam recall + contact lens reorder reminders. Similar recall model to veterinary — predictable annual cycle, easy automation win. Contact lens reorders are a recurring revenue opportunity most practices handle manually or not at all.

**Best time:** 10am–noon. Optometry offices are often less front-heavy than dental — you may get the doctor or office manager in 2 minutes.

## Target 7: Independent CPA or Bookkeeping Firm — Allen

**Search:** "CPA Allen TX" or "bookkeeper Allen TX" — look for small independent firms (2–10 employees), not H&R Block or national chains.

**What to look for:** End of tax season (May 1 is literally the first day after the post-extension rush for many CPAs). This is the best possible time to walk into a CPA — they've just surfaced from six weeks of chaos and are thinking about what their systems need.

**Opener:** "I know you just came out of tax season — what does the annual review scheduling process look like for your ongoing business clients? Do you reach back out proactively, or do they mostly come to you?"

**Hook:** Annual client review scheduling + referral request automation. CPAs have deeply trusted relationships with small business clients and almost never systematically ask for referrals. Post-tax season is the highest-trust window of the year.

**Best time:** 10am–noon Friday. This is the first full week post-April 30. CPAs are catching their breath and are mentally open to conversations about building better systems.

## Zone Notes

- **Allen:** More grid-planned, strip-mall heavy. Best targets are along US-75 service roads and Exchange Pkwy corridor. Parking is easy.
- **McKinney:** Historic downtown McKinney (Virginia St area) has independent boutique businesses, restaurants, and service providers in a walkable district. Worth a separate loop from the US-75 strip.
- **Drive time from Dallas:** 35–45 minutes north on US-75 without traffic. Leave by 9am to hit Allen first, then McKinney.
