## One Insight

The dental blog post opens a new walk-in vertical that's worth thinking about strategically. Dental practices are appointment-based businesses with a very specific, quantifiable problem (recall drop-off, open treatment plans) and a decision-maker who is on-site and accessible. A dentist is harder to cold-call than a plumber — but easier to walk into during a slow mid-afternoon.

The pitch to a dentist is also more precise than the home service pitch, which helps: "You have diagnosed treatment in your system that hasn't been scheduled. I build the sequence that closes it." A dentist knows exactly what that means. They know exactly how much money it represents. And unlike home service owners, they're often not being pitched by anyone doing what Virdar does.

Think about adding 2 to 3 dental walk-ins per week — one after lunch on a Wednesday or Thursday when the morning schedule is done and the dentist has a few minutes. Lower volume than home service walk-ins but potentially higher average project value.
