# LinkedIn Post — Friday, May 22, 2026

**Topic:** The declined service / estimate follow-up gap
**Target length:** Under 200 words
**Best time to post:** 7–8am CT

---

Every roofing contractor who worked through hail season has 20+ estimates sitting unfollowed.

Every auto shop has declined services — brakes, tires, worn belts — that the customer said "not today" to and never heard about again.

Every HVAC company has quotes that went out in March and got one follow-up call before the tech moved to the next job.

The homeowner who didn't call back isn't always choosing someone else. They're waiting for whoever follows up first. After three weeks with no contact, they assume you're too busy to care.

The problem isn't closing ability. It's contact volume. You can call six people back personally when you're also running two crews and managing a full schedule. You can't call thirty.

A systematic follow-up sequence contacts all thirty. Day 3, Day 7, Day 14 — messages specific to the estimate or service, with a clear next step. The owner gets flagged when someone replies. Everyone else stays in sequence until they respond or time out.

The revenue is in the work you already quoted. Most businesses just don't have a system that reaches back out.
