## One Insight

The Southlake/Colleyville market is the right zone for a different reason than the others.

Every other walk-in zone has been about businesses losing revenue to operational gaps — declined services, missed appointments, lapsed customers, slow lead response. The pitch is: here's money your business is losing, here's the system that stops the leak.

Financial advisors, insurance agents, and CPAs in Southlake have a different problem. They're not obviously losing money. Their practice is stable, clients are retained at a reasonable rate, and revenue is consistent. The gap is invisible: the referrals they didn't generate, the clients who drifted slowly rather than leaving loudly, the annual review conversations that didn't happen. The opportunity cost is real — $48,000/year in referral revenue for a 100-client RIA is a number that's easy to calculate — but it requires a different kind of conversation to surface.

The opener for this market is a question, not a statement. "When a client hits a milestone, does your firm have a systematic way to ask for an introduction at that exact moment?" Most advisors will say no, and then they'll pause, because they know what the right answer should have been. That pause is the opening.

The close for this market is also different. These buyers are more analytical, more skeptical of vendors, and more likely to want to think before they commit. Build in an explicit "think about it" step: "I'll send you a one-pager with the math specific to a practice your size. You don't have to do anything with it right now. But if it's real, the conversation is worth 30 minutes." That's the Calendly close — not today's yes, but a scheduled conversation that happens later.
