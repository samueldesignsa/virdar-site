**Topic:** Lapsed client reactivation — the dormant database problem

---

Pull up your booking system and run this filter: clients who came in at least once, last appointment more than 90 days ago.

What's the count?

For a typical appointment-based business with 300 clients in the system, that list runs 80 to 150 people. At a $200 average ticket, that's $16,000 to $30,000 in revenue that came in, had a good experience, and then heard nothing for three months.

They didn't leave because something went wrong. Life moved on. Nobody reached back out.

A single reactivation message — personal, specific to their last visit, with a direct booking link — converts 10 to 20 percent of that list. Not because it's clever marketing. Because most people on that list would come back if someone just asked them to.

A quarterly sweep of your lapsed client database, run automatically, is the highest-return outreach most appointment-based businesses will ever do. No ad spend. No new leads. Just people who already trust you, already had a good experience, and are waiting to hear from you.

Most never do.

What does your lapsed client follow-up look like right now?
