# LinkedIn Post — Monday, May 18, 2026

**Post this Monday morning before 9am CT. Best window: 7–9am CT.**

---

A homeowner calls your roofing company at 8:47pm on a Tuesday.

They just noticed soffit damage. They want someone out this week before the rain forecast. They're going to call whoever gets back to them first.

Your office is closed. The call goes to voicemail.

By 9am Wednesday, they've already gotten a callback from two other companies. One of them scheduled an inspection for Thursday morning while they were still on the phone.

You call at 9:15am. "We already have someone coming out."

This isn't a sales problem. It's a timing problem. The lead was real. The job was there. The window was three hours.

40% of service business inquiries come in outside business hours. Most businesses return those calls the next morning, by which point the lead has either booked with someone else or gone cold overnight.

An automated response at 8:47pm doesn't close the job. But it confirms you got the message, it sets expectations, and it gives them a way to schedule or share details while the urgency is still live.

The morning callback now lands differently. "We saw your message last night — let me get you on the schedule."

Speed-to-response is the most measurable sales variable in service businesses. It's also one of the easiest to fix.
