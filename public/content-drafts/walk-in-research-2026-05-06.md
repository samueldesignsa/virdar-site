# Walk-In Research — Wednesday, May 6, 2026

## Zone: Frisco / Allen / McKinney — Boutique Fitness Corridor
**Target zip codes:** 75034, 75035, 75002, 75013, 75069, 75070

---

### Why this zone today

Frisco and Allen are the densest boutique fitness markets in DFW outside of Uptown Dallas. The Preston Road / Legacy Drive / Stacy Road corridor running from Frisco south through Allen into McKinney has a high concentration of franchise-owned boutique studios — Pilates, barre, cycling, HIIT — that are individually owned small businesses with 200–500 active members and limited staff. These owners run everything: they teach classes, handle the front desk, manage billing, and try to keep members engaged. They don't have an operations director. They have a group fitness management platform (Mindbody, Glofox, Pike13) that has built-in messaging tools they haven't configured.

The pitch is different from professional services. These are cash-flow businesses with tight margins and a specific, measurable leak: member churn. The average boutique studio loses 5–8% of its member base per month. At $130/member, a 300-person studio is losing $19,500–$31,200/month to churn. Most owners know their total cancellations. Almost none know what their at-risk members look like before they cancel.

## Target 1: Club Pilates — Frisco / Preston Rd Area

**Find it:** Search "Club Pilates Frisco" on Google Maps. Multiple locations in the 75034/75035 zip. Preston Rd corridor near Eldorado Pkwy is the most established.

**What they do:** Reformer Pilates franchise, individually owner-operated. Typical membership: $149–$199/month for 8–12 classes. Studio size: 15–25 reformers. Average 200–350 active members.

**What I noticed:** Club Pilates locations run on Mindbody, which has a messaging module that most franchise owners haven't set up for automated sequences. The franchise provides some materials, but the at-risk member detection, new member onboarding, and lapsed member reactivation are all left to the individual owner. The "active" member count in their system includes everyone who hasn't formally cancelled — including members who haven't attended in 6 weeks.

**Hook:** "What percentage of your active members actually came in at least once last week?" Most owners will say they don't know off the top of their head. "If it's under 70%, you've got members who are paying but drifting — and they're about 4 to 6 weeks from cancelling. Does your system flag that?" Let them answer. Almost all of them will say no.

## Target 2: Pure Barre — Allen / Stacy Rd or Exchange Pkwy Area

**Find it:** Search "Pure Barre Allen TX" on Google Maps. Allen has at least one franchised location near the Stacy Rd / US-75 corridor (75002).

**What they do:** Barre technique fitness franchise. Monthly membership model: $139–$169/month. Typical studio: 25–40 members per class, 15–20 classes/week. Classes book through the Pure Barre app — which is separate from their back-end member management.

**What I noticed:** Pure Barre Allen likely runs an intro offer — first week free or first month discounted — that converts somewhere between 35–50% to full membership. The gap between 35% and 50% on 20 new trial offers/month is 3 additional members × $150/month × 10-month avg tenure = $4,500/month in LTV. No paid advertising required — just a better follow-up sequence during the trial week.

**Hook:** "When someone buys an intro offer, what does your follow-up look like during that first week to move them to a full membership?" Most owners will describe something informal — they try to greet them at the desk, maybe send one email. "What if the sequence ran automatically — day 1, day 3, day 5, day 7 — specific to what they said they were looking for when they signed up?" Let them react.

## Target 3: F45 Training — McKinney / S Stacy Rd Area

**Find it:** Search "F45 Training McKinney TX" on Google Maps. McKinney has locations in the southern part of the city near the Allen border (75070).

**What they do:** High-intensity functional training in 45-minute group sessions. Membership model: $200–$250/month. Typically 15–20 members per session, 20–25 sessions/week. Heavy on community identity — members often come 4–5 days/week.

**What I noticed:** F45's strength is also its retention risk. Members come in at high frequency in their first 3 months, then life events (travel, work schedule change, minor injury) disrupt the habit. When an F45 member drops from 5 sessions/week to 1 session/week, that's a clear pre-cancellation signal that happens over 2–3 weeks. The studio sees it in attendance data. Nobody acts on it because the owner is teaching most of the sessions.

**Hook:** "When a member who was coming in four or five times a week suddenly drops to once — or stops showing up entirely — does your system flag that for you?" Almost certainly no. "That window — week three of low attendance through week six — is when you can save the membership. After that, they've mentally moved on." The at-risk detection story lands well here because the drop in frequency is so visible in F45's model.

## Target 4: CycleBar — Frisco / Legacy Dr Area

**Find it:** Search "CycleBar Frisco TX" on Google Maps. Look for the location near Legacy Dr / Preston Rd in Frisco (75033/75034).

**What they do:** Premium indoor cycling franchise. Class-pack and membership model. Signature experience: dim lighting, music-driven, 45-minute high-energy ride. Classes run at 35–45 riders. Strong waitlist culture — popular time slots (6am, 5:30pm, Saturday 8am) book out days in advance.

**What I noticed:** CycleBar waitlist management is a known friction point. When a late cancel happens — and it happens on every popular class — the system sends a Mindbody notification to everyone on the waitlist simultaneously. The first person to claim it gets in. This is fine in theory, but in practice many waitlisted members don't catch the notification in time, the spot sits empty, and the class runs short. The late-cancel revenue leak is real.

**Hook:** "When you get a late cancel on a Friday night class that was waitlisted, how long does it take before you know the spot is actually going to be filled?" Most owners will say it depends on whether the waitlist person responds. "What if the system worked the waitlist sequentially — notifies person 1, gives them 10 minutes to claim it, moves to person 2 if they don't respond — so the spot gets filled before the class starts?" Clear operational improvement, easy to visualize.

## Target 5: Orangetheory Fitness — Frisco or Allen (Franchise Location)

**Find it:** Search "Orangetheory Fitness Frisco TX" or "Orangetheory Fitness Allen TX" on Google Maps. Multiple franchise-owned locations in both cities. Look for a standalone location with a clearly franchisee-operated feel (not inside a shopping center managed by a larger operator).

**What they do:** Heart rate-based interval training in 60-minute group classes. Membership tiers: $59–$159/month depending on class frequency. Studio size: 25–30 stations. High-volume classes, strong brand loyalty.

**What I noticed:** Orangetheory has a corporate tech stack that franchisees are required to use, which limits some automation options. But individual franchise owners still control their own member outreach for local campaigns, lapsed member follow-up, and personal training add-ons (Orangetheory has a "Transformation Challenge" and coaching tier that requires individual-location marketing). The at-risk detection story applies — their corporate app shows attendance, but it doesn't generate proactive outreach to drifting members.

**Hook:** "Does the corporate system ever flag members who haven't come in for 3 weeks and send them an outreach, or is that something you handle individually?" If they handle it individually, the answer is usually "we try to" — which means inconsistently. That's the opening.

**Note:** Corporate franchise constraints may limit what's buildable here. Best to understand their technology situation before going deep on a specific solution. May be better as a relationship-building visit than a hard pitch.

## Target 6: Independent Yoga or Pilates Studio — McKinney Downtown / Historic District

**Find it:** Search "yoga studio McKinney TX" or "Pilates studio McKinney TX" on Google Maps. Filter for independently branded studios (not franchise chains) in or near the historic downtown area (75069).

**What they do:** Yoga, mat Pilates, or mixed wellness studio. Typically 50–150 active members. Membership model: drop-in, monthly unlimited, class packs. Owner often teaches all or most classes. Software: Mindbody or a simpler platform like WellnessLiving.

**Why this target:** Independent studios are the cleanest conversation because there's no corporate layer. The owner is the decision-maker, the one who feels every cancellation personally, and the one who has no marketing budget. The at-risk detection story and the lapsed member reactivation story are both direct hits. Small studio, tight margins, owner-operator — they understand the math immediately.

**Hook:** "Do you have a list of people who used to come in and stopped — not cancelled, but stopped booking?" In Mindbody, that list is easy to pull. Most owners have never actually looked at it. "How many names do you think are on it?" Then: "What if we could put 15% of them back on your schedule in the next 90 days?"

## Logistics for today

**Best time to walk in:** 10am–noon. Studios have morning peak classes that end by 9:15–9:30am. The owner is usually on-site, the morning rush is over, and the next class block doesn't start until 11am or noon. That 90-minute window is when they're actually available.

**Dress code:** Athletic or business casual. Not a suit. These owners are wearing yoga pants and talking about fascia release. Match the energy — approachable, direct, not corporate.

**Leave-behind for every stop:** `virdar.co/blog/ai-automation-for-fitness-studios-gyms.html` — blog post published this morning, specific to their industry.

**What to ask before you launch into the pitch:** "How many active members do you have right now?" Then: "And what does your cancellation rate look like month to month?" Let them tell you the number. When they say 15 to 25 cancellations per month on 300 members, the math is right there: $1,950–$3,250/month walking out. That's the opening.

**Close:** "I'll send you a one-page summary with the math specific to a studio your size. You don't have to do anything with it right now. If the numbers are real for you, it's worth a 30-minute call." Then Calendly.
