## Target 1: Independent CPA / Accounting Firm — Allen (US-75 / Bethany Dr area)

**Search:** "CPA Allen TX" or "accounting firm Allen TX" on Google Maps. Look for firms with a person's name or a local name ("Heritage," "Lone Star," "Allen Financial Group"), 15–60 reviews, been in business 5+ years, handles both individual and small business returns.

**Why now:** We're in mid-May — four weeks past the April 15 deadline. Tax season exhaustion has cleared. The firm is either catching up on extensions or in the quiet stretch before fall planning season starts. This is the window when owners have enough mental bandwidth to talk about systems. In January and March, they're too busy. In late May, they're ready.

**What you'll notice:** The website probably lists services but has no online scheduling for a consultation. No portal mentioned on the homepage. No mention of off-season services like estimated payment planning or bookkeeping support. The firm relies on clients to initiate contact rather than having any proactive outreach running.

**Walk-in approach:** These are typically small practices — 1 CPA plus 1–2 support staff, or a small partnership. Ask for the owner or managing partner directly. Don't pitch to the receptionist.

**Opener:** "I work with independent accounting practices on one specific problem: client communication between filing seasons. Most of the firms I talk to have clients they served reliably for years who quietly stopped coming back — not because anything went wrong, but because they never heard from the firm between April and January. I'm curious: for your personal and small-business clients, what does your off-season outreach look like?"

**The follow-up math:** A 150-client firm that loses 12 clients per year to silent churn (typical for practices with no off-season communication) and retains 5 of them through a structured off-season sequence recovers $4,500 in annual revenue at an $900 average engagement value. Document collection automation saves 2–3 hours of staff time per client across filing season — 300–450 hours at $45/hour is $13,500–$20,250 in recovered capacity per year.

**Reference:** Today's new blog post (`virdar.co/blog/ai-automation-for-accounting-cpa-firms.html`) is a direct leave-behind. It's written specifically for this conversation.

**Best time:** 9:30–11:30am. Mid-morning is the sweet spot — staff is past the morning mail, return prep for extension clients hasn't hit full speed yet.

## Target 2: Independent CPA / Accounting Firm — McKinney (US-380 corridor or historic downtown)

**Search:** "CPA McKinney TX" or "tax preparation McKinney TX" — McKinney has grown fast enough that some of these firms opened in the past 5–8 years and are still in building mode. Those are good prospects. Also check the historic downtown square on Louisiana St and Tennessee St — a few professional service offices are mixed in with the retail and restaurants there.

**What to look for:** A firm that has grown its client count largely through word of mouth and referrals from the residential growth in McKinney. They've been adding clients but haven't built any systems for keeping them. The older book — clients from 2017 or 2018 — is where the silent churn is happening.

**Opener:** "McKinney has added a lot of residents and small businesses in the past several years, which means a lot of accounting firms here grew their client base fast. I'm curious — for the clients you've had for 5+ years, what does your off-season touch point look like? Those are often the highest churn-risk clients because the relationship has shifted to 'I only hear from them when I owe money.'"

**Why this lands:** Any honest accountant will recognize this. The clients who've been around the longest are often the ones who feel most taken for granted — and who are most likely to quietly try something else.

**Best time:** 9:30am–noon. McKinney owners tend to be in early and accessible before lunch.

## Target 3: Independent Auto Repair Shop — McKinney or Allen

**Search:** "auto repair McKinney TX" or "auto repair Allen TX" — look for independent shops (not Firestone, Midas, Jiffy Lube, or NTB). Shops with a person's name or a local name, 40–150 Google reviews, been in business 4+ years. Avoid dealer service departments entirely.

**What you'll notice:** No systematic maintenance interval outreach. A shop that did an oil change and tire rotation for 300 customers in the past year has 300 customers who are each due for their next service in a predictable timeframe. Most of them will go wherever is convenient when they think of it — which might not be this shop.

**Why McKinney/Allen:** This zone has a high density of dual-income households with newer vehicles. The average vehicle age in these zip codes is lower than Garland/Mesquite, which means higher-quality service relationships and more consistent maintenance intervals. These are customers worth keeping.

**Opener:** "Most independent shops I talk to track their service history — they know when a car came in and what was done. What I'm curious about is whether that data is being used to reach out proactively: 'Your oil change from three months ago is due, here's a link to schedule.' If that outreach isn't happening automatically, you're relying on customers to remember to call you."

**The math:** A shop with 300 active customers and a 60% return rate that improves to 73% return rate through maintenance-interval outreach retains 39 additional service visits per year. At $180 average ticket, that's $7,020 in recovered annual revenue from customers the shop already paid to acquire.

## Target 4: HVAC Contractor — McKinney or Allen

**Search:** "HVAC company McKinney TX" or "air conditioning Allen TX" — independently owned contractors, not franchises. Look for trucks with local branding. 4–8 years in business is ideal. 50–200 Google reviews.

**Why now:** This is the highest-leverage time to have this conversation. May in North Texas is pre-peak-cooling-season. The serious heat hits in June. HVAC contractors are busy but not yet slammed. They're booking system tune-ups and pre-summer checks. Their job history from the past 3–5 years is full of customers who should be getting a pre-season tune-up call and aren't.

**Opener:** "You're probably heading into your busiest period in the next 4–6 weeks. I'm curious — for customers who had systems installed or major repairs done in 2021, 2022, 2023, do you have anything running to reach out proactively before the heat hits? Those customers are 3–5 years into a system and are exactly the ones who need a pre-summer check-in."

**The math:** An HVAC contractor with 500 past-service customers that runs a pre-season tune-up campaign and converts 20% to a $185 tune-up generates $18,500 from a list it already owns. Many of those tune-ups also surface system issues that become larger service calls.

**Search:** "optometrist Allen TX" or "eye doctor McKinney TX" — independently owned practices, not LensCrafters, Pearle Vision, or MyEyeDr. franchise locations. Look for practices with 50–150 Google reviews, in operation 5+ years.

**What you'll notice:** No systematic annual exam recall beyond an automated birthday or anniversary email (if that). A practice with 600 active patients that doesn't run proactive recall is losing 150–200 patients per year who don't rebook on their own.

**The math:** A practice with 700 active patients and a 68% annual return rate that improves to 80% through systematic 12-month recall outreach recovers 84 additional exam visits per year. At $195 average exam revenue, that's $16,380 in recovered annual revenue from patients who already trust the practice.

## Target 6: Chiropractic or Physical Therapy Practice — Allen or McKinney

**Search:** "chiropractor Allen TX" or "physical therapy McKinney TX" — independent practices, not large multi-location chains. Look for practices with a single doctor or small partnership, 30–120 reviews, cash-pay or insurance-based.

**What you'll notice:** Patients who completed an initial care plan and never returned. Chiropractic especially has a retention problem: patients come in acute, complete 6–12 visits, feel better, and disappear until the next incident. Practices that run no post-discharge outreach are leaving wellness visit revenue on the table and competing for the same patient at the next acute episode instead of maintaining the relationship in between.

**Opener:** "In chiropractic and PT, the most expensive patient is the one you see once for acute care and then compete for again when the next injury happens. I'm curious — for patients who completed a care plan in the past 12 months and haven't been back, does the practice have any outreach running, or does it rely on them calling when something flares up?"

**The math:** A chiropractic practice with 200 completed-care patients from the past 18 months that converts 15% to a wellness visit at $95/visit generates $2,850 in recurring revenue from patients it otherwise only sees at crisis moments. Compounded over time, the retention math is much larger.

**Best time:** 9–11am. Practices have morning appointment blocks and a mid-morning window between early patients and the midday rush.

## Zone Notes

- **Allen (US-75 / Bethany Dr corridor):** Dense strip of professional service offices — CPAs, financial planners, medical practices, insurance agencies — along and just off the Central Expressway. Walkable between targets in the same strip centers. High density and easy parking.
- **Allen (Main St / McDermott Dr area):** Older, more established commercial strip with a mix of service businesses. More likely to find owner-operated shops and practices that have been there 10+ years.
- **McKinney historic downtown (Louisiana St / Tennessee St):** The highest concentration of owner-operated independent businesses in the zone — boutiques, restaurants, and a scattered set of professional offices. More relationship-oriented than the strip-center businesses. Good for a longer conversation; these owners aren't rushed.
- **McKinney (US-380 / Custer Rd / Hardin Blvd):** Newer commercial development. More corporate-park mix but solid independent coverage in the strip centers. Best for medical, HVAC, and auto repair targets.
- **Drive time from Dallas:** Allen is 25–30 minutes north on US-75. McKinney adds another 10–15 minutes north. Start in Allen for the morning targets and move to McKinney after 10am.
- **Tone for this zone:** Allen/McKinney skews professional and practical. These owners have heard plenty of pitches. Open with a question about their specific process, not a claim about your product. Specific numbers land; vague value propositions don't.
