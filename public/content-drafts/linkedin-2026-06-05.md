# LinkedIn Post — Friday, June 5, 2026

An optometry practice in Murphy has 170 pediatric patients overdue for their annual exam.

None of them have called.

That's not unusual. Families don't track eye exam anniversaries. They mean to go. They get busy. The appointment happens when the practice reaches out first — or when something goes wrong at school and the teacher sends a note home.

August is 8 weeks out. Texas schools start the third week of August. The families scheduling kids' vision checks before school starts are doing it in June and July. By August, the good morning slots are gone and the practice is scrambling to fit in everyone who waited until September.

The practices filling their August calendar are sending messages right now: "Your child is 14 months out from their last exam. We have open mornings in July before the back-to-school rush — want me to lock one in?"

That message goes to 170 patients. About 60 respond. At $235 per exam and average eyewear, that's $39,000 from patients who were already overdue and were going to schedule eventually anyway.

The ones waiting for patients to call fill their August with whoever remembered to schedule.
