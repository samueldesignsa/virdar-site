## Target 1: Independent Dental Practice — MacArthur Blvd / Airport Freeway Corridor

**What they do:** General dentistry — cleanings, restorations, cosmetic work. Solo or two-doctor practice, not an Aspen Dental or DSO.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "dentist Irving TX MacArthur" or "family dental Las Colinas TX." The MacArthur Blvd corridor between Airport Freeway (183) and Rochelle Blvd has several strip center medical suites with independent dental practices. Also check the N MacArthur Blvd area near Northgate Dr. Look for practices with 60–200 Google reviews, local branding (doctor's name or neighborhood name), open 5+ years.

**What to notice:** Irving dental practices often show a review pattern that tells the whole story: consistent 4+ star ratings with the last review from 3+ months ago. That's a practice that has happy patients and no proactive review request system. The recall gap is the same as everywhere — a patient due for a cleaning gets one reminder, doesn't respond, and ages off the active list. For a practice with 700+ active patients, 15% recall improvement is $40–70K in hygiene revenue annually.

Also look for: whether the practice has a "Request Appointment" button on their site that actually works. A surprising number of small practices have a form that no one monitors consistently.

**Hook:** "I work with dental practices on one specific problem — the patient who was due for a cleaning 4 months ago and didn't respond to the first reminder. Most offices I talk to tell me that patient just falls off. I'm curious what your second and third touch looks like for that group, and how many patients are currently sitting in that bucket."

**Revenue math:** A practice with 700 active patients improving annual return rate from 76% to 84% adds 56 hygiene visits per year. At $185 average hygiene visit, that's $10,360 in direct recall revenue — plus downstream restorative treatment booked at those visits.

## Target 2: Independent Insurance Agency — Las Colinas / O'Connor Blvd Area

**What they do:** Independent P&C agency — personal lines (auto, home) and commercial. Not a State Farm or Farmers captive. Independent agent representing multiple carriers.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "independent insurance agent Irving TX" or "insurance agency Las Colinas TX." The O'Connor Blvd corridor and the streets around Las Colinas Blvd have a cluster of small agency offices — many have been there since the early 2000s and have established books of business. Look for 5–60 Google reviews, local or founder-name branding, 8+ years in business.

**What to notice:** Las Colinas agencies built their books during the suburban growth of the 2000s and early 2010s. Many have 250–450 policy books with clients they haven't proactively contacted in years beyond the renewal notice. When premiums jumped in 2022–2024 (especially homeowners), clients shopped and some left. The owners who lost clients are now very aware that passive renewal management doesn't work. That's the opening.

Also check: Their Google review response rate. An agency with 30+ reviews and 5 responses has a visible gap any owner will recognize.

**Hook:** "Independent agencies I talk to in this area built solid books during the growth years — and the challenge now is that proactive client outreach is still manual. The renewal notice goes out, the client sees the premium bump, and if no one has talked to them in 8 months, they shop. I'm curious: in the 45 days before a renewal, what does your outreach sequence look like?"

**Revenue math:** A 350-policy agency at 9% annual lapse improving to 6% through systematic pre-renewal outreach retains 10.5 additional clients per year. At $1,500 average annual premium, that's $15,750 in preserved annual revenue.

## Target 3: Independent Financial Advisor / RIA Firm — Las Colinas Urban Center

**What they do:** Fee-only financial planning, wealth management, investment advisory. Not an Edward Jones or wirehouse branch. Small RIA firm (1–4 advisors) that has been operating independently for 8–20 years.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "financial advisor Las Colinas TX" or "wealth management Irving TX." The Las Colinas Urban Center — around Regent Blvd, Las Colinas Blvd, and the Canals at Las Colinas area — has a dense cluster of professional service office suites. Also check FINRA BrokerCheck or Google for independent RIAs registered in Irving. Look for firms with $30M–$150M AUM, 1–4 advisors, and a client base primarily in North Texas.

**What to notice:** Las Colinas RIAs often serve corporate and executive clients who relocated to DFW during the tech corridor growth of the 2000s. These advisors built strong client relationships but run touchpoints manually. Annual reviews get scheduled late. Referral asks are awkward and inconsistent. Life events (new job, divorce, retirement) get missed unless the client calls in. The advisor managing 55–80 clients manually is the classic target — enough clients that the workload is real, not so many that they have staff managing it.

**Hook:** "Financial advisors I work with in Las Colinas tend to have the same thing in common — strong client relationships and inconsistent systematic touchpoints. Annual reviews that get scheduled 2 months late. Referrals that never get asked for at the right moment. Clients who hit a life event and drift because no one caught it in time. I'm curious: outside the scheduled annual review, what does your proactive mid-year outreach look like?"

**Revenue math:** An advisor with 65 clients who systematically asks for referrals at two or three peak moments per year generates 3–5 additional introductions annually. At average first-year advisory fees for a new DFW executive client, that's $20K–$35K in new revenue the advisor doesn't have to go find.

## Target 4: Physical Therapy Clinic — Airport Freeway / MacArthur Corridor

**What they do:** Outpatient PT — orthopedic, post-surgical, sports medicine. Independent single-location clinic, not a major chain.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "physical therapy Irving TX" or "physical therapy Las Colinas TX." There are several independent PT clinics along the Airport Freeway (183) corridor and MacArthur Blvd. Look for single-location operations with 40–120 reviews, local branding, open 4+ years. Avoid UT Southwestern-affiliated or major chain clinics.

**What to notice:** Mid-plan patient dropout. PT patients who get 4–6 sessions in and stop before completing their care plan are the core revenue gap in independent PT. The clinic loses the remaining sessions, the patient doesn't get the outcome, and — critically — the practice has no systematic re-engagement process beyond someone calling twice and giving up. Insurance billing complexity in PT also means owners are often underwater in admin, which makes a conversation about automation easier.

**Hook:** "Physical therapy practices have a specific revenue gap I see consistently: patients who get a few sessions in and drop off before finishing their care plan. I'm curious — for a patient who was scheduled for 12 sessions and stopped coming after 5, does your clinic have a re-engagement process, or does it depend on who has bandwidth to follow up that week?"

**Revenue math:** A PT clinic with 90 active patients and 14% mid-plan dropout rate recovering half of those dropouts adds 6.3 patients per month. At 7 average remaining sessions at $120/session, that's $5,292/month in recovered revenue.

## Target 5: Med Spa or Aesthetic Clinic — MacArthur Blvd / Las Colinas Area

**What they do:** Injectables, laser treatments, body contouring, facials. Independent practice, not a national franchise.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "med spa Irving TX" or "aesthetic clinic Las Colinas TX." There are several in the MacArthur / 183 area and around the Las Colinas corridor. Look for practices 3+ years in business, 40–120 reviews, 4.5+ stars.

**What to notice:** Neurotoxin rebooking gap. Botox/Dysport clients should return every 10–14 weeks. Most practices rely on clients to call when they're ready. At 10–12 weeks, the toxin is wearing off — and if no one reaches out, the client either books with whoever has an opening or gets captured by a competitor's ad. Check how many reviews mention specific providers by name — that indicates a relationship-driven practice, which means they'd respond to a conversation about systematizing the relationship.

Las Colinas clients tend to be higher-income professionals. Average treatment value is often $500–$700+ vs. suburban averages. Rebooking 24 additional clients per year at that level is $12,000–$16,800 in recovered annual revenue from one automation.

**Hook:** "Med spas have a retention problem that's consistent across the board — clients who had a great first or second visit and then drifted. Usually it's not dissatisfaction. It's that no one followed up at the 10-week mark when the neurotoxin was wearing off. For clients whose last appointment was 90+ days ago and who haven't rebooked, does your practice have an outreach sequence, or does it rely on them calling in?"

**Revenue math:** A med spa with 180 active clients at 70% annual rebooking rate improving to 83% recovers 23 additional visits per year. At $550 average treatment value in Las Colinas, that's $12,650 in recovered revenue.

## Target 6: Auto Repair Shop — Esters Rd / Pioneer Dr / Belt Line (Irving Industrial Corridor)

**What they do:** General auto repair — brakes, diagnostics, oil changes, tires, scheduled maintenance. Independent owner-operator.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "auto repair Irving TX" or "independent auto shop Esters Irving." The Esters Rd and Pioneer Dr corridors south of the airport have a cluster of independent shops serving the Irving residential and commercial base. Also check Belt Line Rd shops south of Airport Freeway. Avoid Firestone, Midas, and Jiffy Lube.

**What to notice:** Two gaps: (1) declined service follow-up — a customer declines a brake recommendation and drives away, and no one follows up; (2) maintenance interval outreach — a customer gets an oil change, and the shop doesn't reach out when the interval is up 3–4 months later. Irving's auto repair market also has a bilingual customer base (the south Irving corridor). If the shop isn't sending Spanish-language follow-up texts to Spanish-speaking customers, that's a practical example you can raise — their automation should match their customer communication style.

**Hook:** "Auto shops I work with have two follow-up gaps that are pretty consistent: declined services that never get revisited, and maintenance intervals that pass without the shop reaching out. I'm curious — for a customer who declined brakes last month and drove away, does your shop have a follow-up process for that job, or does it depend on whether someone has time to call?"

**Revenue math:** A shop doing 45 declined service write-ups monthly at $380 average, converting 18% through systematic follow-up, recovers $35,532 per year in work they already diagnosed.

## Zone Notes

- **Tone:** Irving business owners span a wide range — blue-collar shop owners who respond to direct numbers, Las Colinas professionals who expect a more polished conversation. Read the room. For shops, lead with the revenue gap. For advisors and insurance agents, start with the client relationship and let the revenue math follow.
- **Bilingual opportunity:** South Irving has a significant Spanish-speaking business community. If you walk into a business where the owner or front desk speaks Spanish, acknowledge it directly — Virdar's systems can send bilingual communications. This is a practical differentiator, not a sales pitch.
- **Tuesday energy:** Professionals are accessible. Their Monday firefighting is mostly done; their week isn't full yet. The Las Colinas office targets (advisor, insurance) will give you the most time on a Tuesday.
- **Parking:** MacArthur strip centers have easy lot parking. Las Colinas Urban Center has surface lots and garages. Esters/Pioneer shops have easy pull-in access.
