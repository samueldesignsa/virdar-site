Most service businesses send an estimate and wait.

The customer said they were interested. The quote was fair. Then nothing. So the business owner tells himself the customer went with someone cheaper, moves on, and forgets about it.

The reality: most of those estimates don't die because of price. They die because nobody followed up.

Research across home service industries consistently shows that 60% of unsold estimates never receive a single follow-up contact from the business. The customer was busy when the quote arrived. They needed to talk to their spouse. They got distracted. They meant to call back.

A follow-up sent 48 hours after an estimate closes somewhere between 15% and 30% of quotes that would otherwise go silent. Two messages — not ten, not aggressive, just two — recover a material percentage of revenue that the business already did the work to generate.

The lead came in. The site visit happened. The quote was built. All of that cost time and money. Letting it go silent without a second message is the most expensive thing most service businesses do every week — and the easiest to fix.
