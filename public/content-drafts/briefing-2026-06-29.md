### 1. Blog Post #46 — AI Automation for Martial Arts Schools and Karate Studios (new industry)
**File:** `public/blog/ai-automation-for-martial-arts-schools-karate-studios.html`

New industry, never previously covered. Martial arts schools are a clean fit for Virdar's model — appointment-driven, high lifetime value per student, clear and measurable revenue leaks, and two strong timing angles right now.

**Why this industry, why today:**
- July 4 is 5 days out: the holiday week is the single highest-churn week of the year for recurring-attendance businesses. A student who misses 3–4 consecutive classes over a holiday week doesn't quit — but they break the habit. Without a proactive check-in, that's a quiet dropout.
- Back-to-school enrollment is the biggest revenue event of the year for most schools. Families deciding on fall activities for their kids are doing it in June and July. A school with a July outreach campaign fills fall slots before August. A school that waits fills 20–25% fewer.

**Five problems addressed with specific revenue math:**
1. **Trial → enrollment conversion:** Most schools have no post-trial follow-up. Response speed matters — a 5-minute auto-response vs. next-morning converts at 9x. Structured 3-touch follow-up after the trial turns 40% conversion into 55%: 3 additional students/month = $3,900/year.
2. **Summer dropout:** Proactive check-in after 2 consecutive missed classes + pre-July 4 communication retains 7 of 15 at-risk summer students = $4,550 kept.
3. **Belt test milestone:** A congratulations + prepay offer the evening of the test converts month-to-month students into 6-month commitments at the highest-motivation moment of the year.
4. **Lapsed student reactivation:** 90-day outreach with the specific belt level personalized. 20% reactivation of 40 lapsed students = 8 back × $130 × 8 months = $8,320.
5. **Back-to-school fall enrollment:** Segmented July campaign to past-trial students and lapsed families — 8 additional fall enrollments × $130 × 9 months = $9,360/year.

It's about whether the owner's current system is capturing the revenue their reputation has already earned. That frame works in Arlington.
