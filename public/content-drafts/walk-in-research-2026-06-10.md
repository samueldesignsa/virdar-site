A message to active clients at the 6-month mark — "Is there anyone in your circle who's going through a life transition? ## Zone Notes

- **Addison is not a suburb.** It's a business city. The independent practices here have operated alongside major corporate neighbors for decades and are accustomed to sales calls from service providers. Beverly's opener ("I work with businesses in this area on one specific problem") lands the same way it does in the suburbs — but the business owners here often have more experience with vendors and will ask sharper questions faster. That's good. It means the conversation gets real quickly.

- **Lunch rush is real.** Belt Line Rd from 11:30am to 1pm is the restaurant district's peak. The practices Beverly is targeting are not in the restaurant strip, but parking and foot traffic in the area are at maximum. Plan route around the restaurant cluster, not through it.

"Prospect" instead of "patient," "AUM" instead of "plan revenue," "pipeline bleed" instead of "recall gap." The pitch translates directly once the vocabulary is adjusted.
