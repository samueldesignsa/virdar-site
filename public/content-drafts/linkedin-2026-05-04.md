# LinkedIn Post — Monday, May 4, 2026

**Topic:** The maintenance interval gap — recurring-service businesses have the data to know exactly when each customer is due back, but most wait for the customer to call instead of reaching out first.

**Post for today (Monday). Best window: 7–9am CT or 11am–1pm CT.**

---

Your software already knows when every customer is due back. Most businesses just aren't asking it.

An auto repair shop has every oil change date and mileage in the DMS. A dental practice has every patient's last cleaning and their 6-month recall window. A pest control company knows exactly when each quarterly treatment ran.

Almost none of them use that data to proactively reach out.

The customer doesn't come back because they forgot. Not because of a bad experience. Not because they found someone better. They forgot — and nobody sent a reminder.

The shop that sends "Your Tahoe is coming up on its next service around late May — want to get that on the books?" gets the appointment. The shop that waits gets maybe 60% of its customers back, six weeks late.

This is not a marketing problem. The trigger is predictable. The message writes itself. The customer is already yours.

If you run a recurring-service business and you're waiting for customers to call you, you're collecting roughly 60 cents of every dollar you've already earned. The rest is sitting in your software, waiting to be asked.
