# LinkedIn Post — Wednesday, June 3, 2026

A dental practice in DeSoto runs a recall system that sends one postcard in May.

That's the whole system.

A patient who was due in April gets a single mailer. If they don't call back in two weeks, nothing else happens.

June just started. School's out. The parent who meant to get the kids in "when summer starts" now has a loose intention to call — sitting behind every other item on the summer task list.

The practice that sends a second touch — specific slot, both kids on the same morning — books those appointments. The postcard practice does not.

This isn't a technology problem. It's timing and specificity.

"We have Tuesday the 10th at 9am — we can do both kids back-to-back" is different from "time for your checkup."

The first message removes the friction of scheduling. The second one adds to the parent's to-do list.

Summer is the widest recall gap of the year for a family dental practice. The window is right now — patients are available and waiting for something specific to respond to.
