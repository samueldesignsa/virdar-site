# LinkedIn Post — Monday, June 15, 2026

Every June, independent business owners tell me their slow July is inevitable.

A painting contractor in Plano told me last week he's always slow in summer. A landscaper in Richardson said July is just quiet. A dental office manager said they always have gaps the week of July 4.

None of them are slow because demand dropped.

They're slow because nobody reached the people who said they'd call "later this summer." Those customers are still in the system. They haven't gone to a competitor. They just haven't been contacted.

The painting contractor has 35 past customers who got an estimate in April and went quiet. The landscaper has 50 customers who said they'd schedule service "sometime in the summer." The dental office has 80 appointments booked for the week of July 4 and hasn't confirmed a single one — which means 18 of them will cancel by June 26 when families finalize vacation plans.

The businesses that stay busy in July reached out in May and June. The ones with slow Julys didn't.

A slow summer isn't a market condition. It's a follow-up problem disguised as one.
