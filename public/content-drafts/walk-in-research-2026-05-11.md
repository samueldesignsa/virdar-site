## Target 1: Independent Insurance Agency — Frisco (Preston Rd or Legacy Dr)

**Search:** "independent insurance agency Frisco TX" on Google Maps. Filter for non-captive agencies — avoid State Farm, Farmers, Allstate branch offices. Look for:
- Agency name that includes a person's name or a local geographic name ("Apex," "Summit," "Lone Star," "Legacy")
- 20–80 Google reviews, 4.5+ rating, active for 5+ years
- Handles both personal lines and small commercial

**What you'll likely notice:** Their Google listing hasn't had a new review in 3–4 months. The website mentions "personal service" but there's no clear description of how they stay in contact with clients between renewals. No online scheduling — contact form only or a phone number.

**Walk-in approach:** These are owner-operated shops, usually 2–5 people. Ask to speak to the owner or principal producer. Don't pitch to a CSR.

**Opener:** "I work with independent agencies on one specific problem: keeping clients from shopping at renewal. Most agencies I talk to are losing 10–15% of their personal-lines book every year to competitors who called right before the renewal notice landed. I'm curious — what does your process look like for proactive outreach in the 45 days before a policy renews?"

**The follow-up math:** A 400-client personal-lines agency running systematic 45-day renewal outreach retains 12–16 additional clients per year versus reactive renewal handling. At $1,100 average annual premium, that's $13,000–$18,000 in preserved annual revenue. Show that number — it's specific enough to land.

**Hook from today's blog post:** The renewal gap. Clients who get the carrier notice before the agent calls are already in shopping mode. The agency that reaches out 45 days ahead controls the conversation. It's not about being pushy — it's about being first.

**Best time:** 9:30–11am. Producers are past their morning emails but not yet in afternoon client calls.

## Target 2: Independent Insurance Agency — Prosper (US-380 or Preston Rd near 380)

**Search:** "insurance agency Prosper TX" or "independent insurance Prosper TX" — Prosper is slightly less saturated than Frisco, which means the agencies there have less competition but also fewer sophisticated systems. They've grown their book largely on referrals from new homeowners (Prosper had massive residential growth 2019–2024).

**What to look for:** Agency that opened 3–7 years ago, growing fast, probably 1–2 producers plus support staff. They're busy. They're not thinking about retention yet because they've been adding new clients consistently. But the early clients from 2018–2020 are now the renewal-risk clients — policies that came in at low rates, renewals that have gone up, clients who have never heard from the agency except at claim time.

**Opener:** "Agencies that opened during the Prosper growth boom acquired a lot of clients fast. I'm curious what your book looks like in terms of tenure — do you have a lot of clients from 5+ years ago who haven't been proactively contacted in a while? That's usually where the retention leak is."

**Why this works:** It's not an accusation. It's a question that any honest agency owner will recognize. The answer almost always leads to "yeah, we've been meaning to do something about that."

**Best time:** 9–11am. This is a Monday-morning-energy market — owners are in planning mode.

## Target 3: Independent Optometry Practice — Frisco

**Search:** "optometrist Frisco TX" — look for independently owned practices, not LensCrafters, Pearle Vision, or MyEyeDr. franchise locations. Filter for practices with 40–150 Google reviews, independent branding.

**What you'll notice:** No systematic annual exam recall. Optometry has the same predictable recall cycle as dental (1–2 years for adults, annual for children) but most practices rely on patients to self-schedule. A practice with 800 active patients and a 65% annual return rate is leaving 280 patients per year who don't come back on their own — and nobody is following up.

**Opener:** "I work with independent medical practices on patient recall automation. Optometry has a natural advantage over most specialties — your patients are on a predictable return interval. I'm curious: for patients whose last exam was 12–14 months ago, what does your outreach process look like beyond the standard automated reminder?"

**Hook:** The recall gap. A practice with 800 active patients and 65% return rate that improves to 78% return rate through systematic 12-month recall outreach recovers 104 additional exam visits per year. At $185 average exam revenue, that's $19,000 in recovered annual revenue from patients who already trust the practice.

**Best time:** 10–11:30am on Monday. The practice is running but not yet in the afternoon rush.

## Target 4: Home Services Contractor (Roofing or HVAC) — Frisco/Prosper

**Search:** "roofing company Frisco TX" or "HVAC Frisco TX" — look for independently owned contractors with 50–200 reviews, trucks with local branding, been in business 4+ years. Avoid national franchise contractors (One Hour, Service Champions, etc.).

**What to look for:** Owner-operated shop, 3–12 field techs, service primarily in Frisco/Prosper/McKinney area. These contractors did extremely well during the 2021–2023 hail season in North Texas. They have large databases of past customers who've never been contacted for maintenance, referrals, or follow-on work.

**What you'll notice:** No maintenance plan outreach. A roofing contractor who installed 200 roofs in 2022 has 200 homeowners who will need a 5-year inspection conversation starting this year. None of them are getting a proactive call. The same for HVAC — every customer who bought a new system 3–5 years ago is due for a relationship touchpoint before a competitor's truck shows up in their neighborhood.

**Opener (roofing):** "Most roofing contractors I talk to did a lot of installs in 2021 and 2022 from the storm season. I'm curious — those homeowners are 3–5 years into a new roof now. Do you have anything running to stay in front of them before they need their next inspection or a neighbor asks for a recommendation?"

**Opener (HVAC):** "For HVAC companies that installed systems in the past 3–5 years, a maintenance agreement outreach before summer is one of the highest-return uses of a past-customer list. What does your process look like for reaching out to past install customers before cooling season?"

**Hook:** Past-customer reactivation. A roofing contractor with 200 installs from 2021–2022 who runs a 5-year inspection campaign converts 25–35% into a paid inspection or warranty check at $250 average. That's $12,500–$17,500 from a list the business already owns.

**Best time:** 8–10am. Owner/operators are often at the shop before the field crews head out.

## Target 5: Med Spa or Aesthetic Clinic — Frisco

**Search:** "med spa Frisco TX" — this market is saturated with med spas, which means the established ones are competing for the same client base. Look for independently owned practices (not franchise: not Skin Laundry, Bluemercury, etc.), 50–120 reviews, been open 3+ years.

**What to look for:** Practice running injectables, laser, or body contouring. These clients return every 3–6 months for maintenance treatments. Most practices have no rebooking automation — they depend on clients to call when they feel like it, which means 30–40% of clients who should be returning aren't.

**Opener:** "In aesthetics, the rebooking window is one of the tightest in any service business — clients who delay their next neurotoxin appointment by 2–3 months are the most likely to let it lapse entirely. I'm curious: for clients whose last appointment was 90 days ago, does your practice have an outreach sequence running, or does it depend on the client reaching out?"

**Hook:** Rebooking automation. A med spa with 300 active clients and 70% rebooking rate that improves to 83% recovers 39 additional treatment visits per year. At $425 average treatment value, that's $16,000 in recovered annual revenue from the existing client base.

**Best time:** 10am–noon. Aesthetic clients book morning appointments; the front desk has a window mid-morning.

## Target 6: Fitness Studio or Personal Training Gym — Frisco

**Search:** "personal training studio Frisco TX" or "fitness studio Frisco TX" — look for independent studios (not Planet Fitness, Orangetheory, or F45 franchise locations). Boutique gyms: pilates, strength training, small-group HIIT. These are owner-operated businesses with 20–150 members and high churn.

**What to look for:** Studio that's been open 3+ years, has a membership model, and likely has a churn problem they've never quantified. Member retention is the single biggest financial lever in a boutique studio.

**Opener:** "Boutique fitness has a churn problem that most studios don't measure directly — members who stop attending but haven't cancelled yet. I work with fitness studios on attendance-triggered outreach: when a member's attendance drops below a threshold, it triggers a check-in before they ghost the membership. I'm curious if you have anything like that running, or if that gap is familiar."

**Hook:** Attendance-triggered churn prevention. A studio with 80 active members that loses 25% annually through ghosted memberships and cancellations — typical for boutique fitness — that reduces churn to 18% retains 6 additional members per year. At $175/month average, that's $12,600 in annual revenue recovered from members the studio already acquired.

**Reference:** Today's fitness blog post (`virdar.co/blog/ai-automation-for-fitness-studios-gyms.html`) is a direct leave-behind.

**Best time:** 9–10:30am. Studios typically have a mid-morning lull between early classes and the lunch crowd.
