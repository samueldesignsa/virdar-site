## One Insight

The Lewisville/Flower Mound/Coppell zone is a good test case for a pivot in the walk-in pitch.

In HEB and Grand Prairie, the opener was the cost of the problem: "Here's what the declined service is costing your shop per week." In Flower Mound and Coppell, the more effective opener is probably the cost of inaction over time: "Here's what your past customers are worth if you stay in contact with them — and here's what they cost you if you don't."

The difference is the buyer archetype. An HEB auto shop owner is optimizing for weekly cash flow. A Flower Mound physical therapist or Coppell insurance agent is managing a multi-year book of relationships. The HEB version of the pitch focuses on the immediate gap; the Flower Mound version focuses on the relationship value over 3–5 years that leaks away without a system.

Same problem. Different frame. Worth testing both in the same zone this week to see which gets a more engaged response.
