# LinkedIn Post — Wednesday, May 27, 2026

**Topic:** The estimate graveyard — the quotes you sent that you never followed up on
**Target length:** Under 200 words
**Best time to post:** 7–8am CT or 10–11am CT

---

There's a folder in every trade company's CRM — or just a mental pile — of estimates sent and never answered.

You wrote the quote. The homeowner seemed interested. You sent it over. Nothing came back.

So you moved on. The next job came in. You told yourself you'd follow up when things slowed down.

Things didn't slow down.

That homeowner wasn't necessarily uninterested. They were busy. Or they got three quotes and forgot to cancel the others after they hired someone. Or they're waiting to decide after the school year ends.

A single follow-up message — sent automatically 48 hours after a quote with no response — converts a real percentage of quotes that would have gone dark. Not a sales pitch. Just: "Wanted to check in on the quote we sent Tuesday. We're booking June now if you'd like to lock in dates."

The estimate graveyard isn't a lead problem. It's a follow-up problem. And it's fixable.

**CTA:** How many unanswered quotes do you have in the last 60 days?
