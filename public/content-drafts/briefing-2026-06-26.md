### 1. Blog Post #45 — AI Automation for Dog Grooming Businesses (new industry)
**File:** `public/blog/ai-automation-for-dog-grooming-businesses.html`

New industry, not previously covered in any format. Dog grooming is an appointment-heavy, relationship-driven business with very clear and demonstrable revenue leaks — and July 4 creates a natural, timely hook this week.

The core problems for independent groomers:
- **No-shows:** A groomer with 8 slots per day losing 1.5 to no-shows is missing ~$19,250/year in appointments already on the calendar. A 48-hour confirmation text with a response request ("reply RESCHEDULE if you need a different time") cuts that by 60–70%.
- **Rebooking gap:** Most groomers rely on clients to rebook. At 5 weeks, the owner has noticed the dog needs grooming but hasn't called yet. That's the window. A proactive message at 5 weeks captures clients that would otherwise drift to 10–12 week gaps.
- **Lapsed clients:** Every grooming shop has 20–40 clients who fell off the schedule. A check-in message — personalized with the dog's name — reactivates 15–20% of them. These are not new customers; they already trust the shop.
- **July 4 hook:** Dogs and fireworks anxiety is top-of-mind for pet owners right now. A pre-holiday message to active clients about a de-stress groom before the weekend converts at the highest rate of the year — and lands this week when it's actionable.
- **Reviews:** Groomers with 10 years of loyal clients often have 30 Google reviews. A same-day review request while the owner is looking at their freshly groomed dog generates 2–3 reviews per week. At that rate, a shop reaches 150+ reviews within a year — enough to dominate local search.

Revenue math in the post is specific: $19,250/year from no-show reduction, $2,100/year from rebooking, $2,100 in one week from a pre-July 4 message to 80 active clients.
