# LinkedIn Post — Monday, May 25, 2026

**Topic:** The summer capacity trap — when being busy becomes the reason nothing improves
**Target length:** Under 200 words
**Best time to post:** 7–8am CT (or mid-morning if delayed for Memorial Day)

---

Pool companies, HVAC companies, landscapers, roofing contractors: this week is when you realize summer is actually here.

The calendar fills up. You stop answering the phone as fast. Estimates go out and don't get a second follow-up because you're already booked. The spring outreach campaign you meant to send in March finally doesn't matter because you're swamped anyway.

And then September arrives. The rush ends. You look at the numbers and realize: you were at capacity for three months and revenue still didn't grow.

That's the capacity trap. Being busy does not mean you captured everything available. It means you captured what showed up and stayed in front of you. The lead that came in at 8pm on a Wednesday and got a callback 16 hours later — that job went to someone else. The estimate from April that got one follow-up call — that homeowner signed with a competitor.

The summer rush is the best time to build the system. Because when it ends, you want to look back at a season you actually captured.

**CTA:** What's the gap in your operation that summer makes worse?
