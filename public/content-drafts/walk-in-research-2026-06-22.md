## Zone Notes

**Lower Greenville on a Monday morning is active.** Owners are back from the weekend, fresh, and the week hasn't gotten away from them yet. Monday is a better cold walk-in day than Friday (when owners are wrapping up and mentally checked out) and better than mid-week (when the schedule is packed). First hour after open (9-10am) is the sweet spot.

**The M Streets demographic skews younger than Park Cities or Southlake.** Business owners here are often 35-45, more receptive to technology conversations, less allergic to software and automation. The pitch doesn't need to start with "here's what AI means for your business" — it can start straight with the revenue math. These owners speak numbers.

**July 4 urgency still works — barely.** With 12 days to go, the pre-holiday window is real but tight. After this week (June 22-26), the calendar for July 4 week fills on its own and the outreach does nothing. Beverly should lead with the July 4 hook where it fits (salons, fitness, vet boarding) and transition to the summer reactivation hook (med spa, dental, chiro) where the July 4 angle doesn't apply.

**New med spa leave-behind is live.** Beverly can walk into any Lower Greenville aesthetic practice today with a post published this morning about summer lapsed clients. Same approach as the salon leave-behind Friday — "I published this at 6am based on what I see in the practices I work with. July 4 is 12 days out, and after the holiday your August calendar depends on whether you reached your lapsed clients this week."

**Three solid conversations are better than seven rushed ones.** Lower Greenville is walkable but the strip is long. Beverly should plan for 3-4 real conversations over 3 hours rather than 7 quick pitches with no depth.
