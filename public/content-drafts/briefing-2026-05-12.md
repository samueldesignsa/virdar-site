## One Insight

The CPA conversation has a specific advantage over most walk-ins: the timing is self-evident to the owner. They just finished the hardest 10 weeks of their year. The pain of document chasing, client communication gaps, and losing clients who never said goodbye is fresh. You're not asking them to imagine a problem — you're asking about the one they just lived through.

The line that tends to work: "What does your off-season outreach look like?" Most will pause. The honest answer is "we don't really have one." That pause is the opening.

The HVAC conversation has a different but equally strong timing signal: pre-season urgency. These owners know June is coming. They know their job is to be the contractor customers call first when the AC dies. Any system that keeps them in front of past customers before that moment — without requiring staff to manage it — is an obvious yes if the math works.

Two strong verticals, two different timing hooks. Today is a good day to walk in.
