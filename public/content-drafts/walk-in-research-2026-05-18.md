## Target 1: Independent Dental Practice — Campbell Road Corridor

**What they do:** General dentistry. Cleanings, restorations, cosmetic work. Solo or two-doctor practice. Not Aspen Dental or a DSO.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "dentist Richardson TX Campbell Road" or "family dental Richardson TX." The stretch of Campbell Rd between US-75 and Coit Rd has at least 6–8 independent dental practices in strip centers and medical office buildings. Look for practices with 80–200 Google reviews, a local or doctor-name brand, been open 5+ years.

**What to notice:** Most practices in this corridor still run recall on a single-reminder system. Patient due for a cleaning gets one postcard or one automated text. If they don't respond, nothing else happens and the patient ages out. Given that dental recall revenue is the bedrock of practice cash flow (hygiene visits leading to restorative treatment), a 15% improvement in recall conversion is material — usually $30–60K per year for a practice with 600+ active patients.

Also check: Google review responses. Practices that have 20+ reviews but only respond to 2 of them are leaving a visible gap any owner will recognize.

**Hook:** "Dental practices in Richardson tend to have a specific gap I see a lot — the patient who's 14 months overdue for a cleaning and didn't respond to the first reminder. Most offices tell me that patient just falls off the list. I'm curious what your second and third outreach looks like for that group."

**Revenue math:** A practice with 600 active patients at 78% annual return rate improving to 85% through multi-touch recall adds 42 hygiene visits per year. At $200 average hygiene visit, that's $8,400 in direct recall revenue — plus downstream restorative treatment from the visits.

## Target 2: Independent Auto Repair Shop — Belt Line Road / Arapaho Area

**What they do:** General auto repair — oil changes, brakes, tires, diagnostics, scheduled maintenance. Independent owner-operator.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "auto repair Richardson TX" or "independent auto shop Belt Line Richardson." The Belt Line Road corridor (east of US-75) and the Arapaho Road / Spring Valley area have multiple independent shops. Look for 4.0–4.5 star rating, 50+ reviews, been open 4+ years. Avoid Firestone, Midas, or Jiffy Lube.

**What to notice:** Two gaps common to independent shops in this area:
1. **Declined service follow-up**: A customer declines a brake recommendation and drives away. Does the shop contact them 2–3 weeks later? Almost always no.
2. **Maintenance interval outreach**: A customer gets an oil change in January. Does anyone reach out in April when the interval is up? Usually not unless the customer calls.

Also check: How many Google reviews mention waiting for a callback or not hearing back. That's a visible sign of a communication gap the owner already knows about.

**Hook:** "Auto shops I work with have two follow-up gaps that are pretty consistent: declined services that never get revisited, and maintenance intervals that pass without the shop reaching out. I'm curious — for a customer who declined brakes in March and drove away, does your shop have a follow-up process for that, or does it depend on whether someone has time?"

**Revenue math:** A shop that does 40 declined service write-ups per month at $350 average, converting 18% through systematic follow-up, recovers $30,240 per year in jobs they already diagnosed.

**Best time:** 8–10am. Shop owners are in before the bays fill. After 10am is harder to get attention.

## Target 3: Independent Insurance Agency — Spring Valley / Greenville Area

**What they do:** Independent P&C agency — personal lines (auto, home) and commercial. Not a State Farm or Farmers captive. Independent agent representing multiple carriers.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "independent insurance agent Richardson TX" or "insurance agency Spring Valley Richardson." The Spring Valley / Greenville Ave corridor and the US-75 service road between Belt Line and Arapaho have several independent agency offices. Look for small local operations, 10–80 Google reviews, 5+ years in business.

**What to notice:** Same pattern as Plano — Richardson has grown significantly in the past decade, and agencies that added clients during that growth period have back-books of customers who've never been proactively contacted between renewals. Reviews will often show a gap: a few strong reviews at launch, then nothing recent. That's a signal.

Also check: Their website for any online quoting tool or contact form. If there's a form and no one's following up on web leads within the hour, that's a direct conversation starter.

**Hook:** "Independent agencies I talk to in this area often have the same thing in common — a book of clients from the growth years who've never been contacted between renewals. The renewal notice arrives, they see the premium bump, and they shop. I'm curious: in the 45 days before a renewal, what does your proactive outreach look like?"

**Revenue math:** An agency with a 350-policy book at 10% annual lapse rate improving to 7% through pre-renewal outreach retains 10.5 additional clients per year. At $1,400 average annual premium, that's $14,700 in preserved annual revenue.

## Target 4: Physical Therapy or Chiropractic Practice — Multiple Locations

**What they do:** PT clinics treating orthopedic injuries, post-surgical rehab, sports medicine. Chiro practices doing spinal manipulation, pain management, wellness care.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "physical therapy Richardson TX" or "chiropractor Richardson TX." There are several independent clinics along Campbell, Belt Line, and the CityLine/190 corridor. Look for single-location clinics with 50–150 reviews, local branding, not part of a major PT chain.

**What to notice:** Patient drop-off. PT patients who have completed 4–6 sessions but haven't finished their prescribed care plan often just stop coming. The clinic loses the remaining sessions, and those patients often return later (to any provider) with the same injury or a worsened one. The practice has no automated way to re-engage patients who go silent mid-plan.

Chiro practices have the same issue with wellness care — the patient on a monthly maintenance plan who misses one visit and then just never reschedules.

**Hook:** "Physical therapy practices have a specific revenue gap I see consistently: patients who get a few sessions in and then drop off before finishing their care plan. Most clinics tell me they try to call, but if no one answers after the first two attempts, that patient is gone. I'm curious — for a patient who was scheduled for 12 sessions and dropped off after 5, does your practice have a re-engagement process, or does it depend on who has bandwidth to follow up?"

**Revenue math:** A PT practice with 80 active patients per month and 15% mid-plan dropout rate recovering half of those dropouts adds 6 patients per month. At 7 average remaining sessions at $120/session, that's $5,040/month in recovered revenue — $60,480/year.

**Best time:** 9am–11am. PT clinics have morning session blocks but often have a desk window around 9–10am before mid-morning gets busy.

## Target 5: Independent Financial Advisor — US-75 / Campbell Corridor

**What they do:** Fee-only financial planning, wealth management, investment advisory for individuals and families. Not an Edward Jones branch or wirehouse.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "financial advisor Richardson TX" or "wealth management Richardson." The US-75 / Campbell intersection has several professional service office buildings with small RIA firms. Also check the Nortel/TI-area office parks along Renner and Plano roads for independent firms that have been around 8–15 years.

**What to notice:** Same pattern as Plano RIAs — great at relationship, weak on systematic touchpoints. Annual reviews scheduled late, referral asks made awkwardly (or not at all), client life events missed because no one tracked them. The advisor who has 60 clients and manages all touchpoints manually is the typical target.

**Hook:** "Financial advisors I work with tend to be strong at the client relationship — and inconsistent on the systematic moments around it. Annual reviews that get scheduled 90 days late, referrals that never get asked for, clients who drift because no one noticed the life event that would have prompted a conversation. I'm curious: outside of the scheduled annual review, what does your mid-year client outreach look like?"

**Revenue math:** An advisor with 65 clients who systematically asks for referrals at peak moments generates 3–5 additional introductions per year. At average first-year fees for a new advisory relationship, that's $18K–$30K in new revenue from a system the advisor doesn't have to execute manually.

## Target 6: Med Spa or Aesthetic Clinic — Greenville Ave / Belt Line Area

**What they do:** Injectables, laser treatments, body contouring, facials. Independent practice — not a national franchise.

**Where to look:** Google Maps — "med spa Richardson TX" or "aesthetic clinic Richardson TX." There are several in the US-75 / Belt Line / Greenville area, plus some along the 190/CityLine corridor. Look for 3+ years in business, 40–150 reviews, 4.5+ stars.

**What to notice:** Rebooking gap. Neurotoxin (Botox, Dysport) clients should return every 10–14 weeks. Most practices rely on clients to call when they're ready. The ones who had a great experience but didn't rebook immediately either forget or get captured by a competitor running ads at week 8. Check: how many reviews mention a specific provider by name — that's a sign the practice is retention-focused, which means they'd respond well to a conversation about systematizing the rebooking.

**Hook:** "Med spas have a specific retention problem — clients who had a great first or second visit and then drifted. Usually it's not dissatisfaction. It's that no one followed up at the 10-week mark when their neurotoxin was wearing off. I'm curious: for clients whose last appointment was 90 days ago and who haven't rebooked, does your practice have an outreach sequence, or does it rely on them calling in?"

**Revenue math:** A med spa with 200 active clients at 68% annual rebooking rate improving to 80% recovers 24 additional treatment visits per year. At $400 average treatment value, that's $9,600 in recovered revenue — plus the downstream spend from retained clients.

## Route

**Morning (8–10am):** Start with auto repair — hit Belt Line or Arapaho shops early before the bays fill. Park once and hit 2–3 shops in a corridor.

**Mid-morning (9:30–11am):** Move to Campbell Road — dental practice and PT clinic. Both are accessible from the same corridor. Park in a strip center and walk from one to the next.

**Late morning (10:30am–12pm):** Insurance agency (Spring Valley / Greenville area), financial advisor (US-75 corridor), med spa if time allows.

**Drive:** Richardson is 20–25 minutes north of Dallas on US-75. Leave by 8am to hit the first shop by 8:30am. The whole zone is compact — Belt Line to Campbell is about a 10-minute drive.

## Zone Notes

- **Tone:** Richardson business owners are pragmatic and slightly more tech-savvy than average DFW suburban SMBs (UTD influence, tech-corridor culture). They respond to specifics and numbers. Don't pitch AI as a concept — pitch the specific gap and the specific number.
- **Monday energy:** Owners are fresh, not yet buried in the week. It's a strong day for a first impression. If they're too busy, they'll say so — most won't be at 8am or 9am.
- **Parking:** Campbell Road strip centers have ample lot parking. Belt Line shops are easy in/out. No prep needed.
- **Dress:** Business casual across all targets. Auto shops are casual; medical offices are slightly more formal. Read the room when you walk in.
