## One Insight

Rowlett and Rockwall together give Beverly a zone split she hasn't had before: a price-sensitive, relationship-first market (Rowlett) right next to a high-income, growth-oriented market (Rockwall) on the same 35-minute drive east on I-30.

The buyer profile is different enough that the same hook needs different framing. In Rowlett — "here's what you're leaving on the table per week" (cost-of-inaction framing). In Rockwall — "here's what your current patients/clients are worth over time" (lifetime value framing). Both are true, but one lands better with an owner who's managed costs carefully for 15 years vs. an owner whose practice is growing and wants to know what's possible.

Beverly's job today isn't to close — it's to have conversations specific enough that a follow-up call feels natural. The best outcome is: they mention a specific number (paused accounts, declined services, overdue recalls), Beverly names a dollar value around that number, and the owner says "huh, I hadn't thought about it that way." That's the moment that produces a strategy call booking.
