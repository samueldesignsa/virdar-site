**Five problems addressed with specific revenue math:**
1. **Inquiry → first lesson conversion:** Same-hour response vs next-morning is a meaningful conversion difference. A parent deciding on piano lessons for their 8-year-old is in decision mode right now. 2 additional students/month × $140 × 10 months = $2,800/year.
2. **Cancellation slot recovery:** 10 cancellations/month, currently filling 1–2 manually. Automated waitlist outreach fills 6–7. 5 additional lessons × $35/lesson × 12 months = $2,100/year recovered.
3. **Spring recital → fall commitment:** Message sent within 48 hours of the recital converts the emotionally committed to the officially enrolled. 88% vs 72% retention across 35 students = $8,400/year.
4. **Summer-to-fall pipeline:** Three-segment outreach (current students, summer trial students, lapsed students) — each needing a different message. Personalized outreach gets 3–4× the response rate of a broadcast email.
5. **Lapsed student reactivation:** 60 lapsed students, 20% return = 12 students × $140 × 9 months = $15,120 recovered.

## One Insight

The last several weeks of walk-in research have been North Dallas suburbs — planned corridors, newer businesses, owners in their 30s and 40s who are comfortable with systems and technology. Fort Worth's Near Southside is a different texture. Magnolia Avenue in particular has businesses that have been operating in the same building for 10–20 years, some of them started by the owner's parent. These owners have a specific form of skepticism: they've seen sales pitches, they've bought software they didn't use, and they've been promised efficiency that didn't arrive.

The frame that works in that environment isn't "here's what AI can do." It's "here's a specific dollar amount your current system is leaving behind, and here's why." The auto repair shop on South Loop 820 doesn't want to hear about automation. They want to hear: "You have customers who said 'not yet' on $600 worth of work in April. They're about to drive those same cars 300 miles on July 4. The shops that follow up on declined services before a holiday recover an average of $4,200 in that week alone." That's the entry point. The system is just how it gets done.

Beverly's credibility with that kind of owner comes from specifics, not concepts. Know the number before walking in. The walk-in research file has industry-specific math for each stop.
