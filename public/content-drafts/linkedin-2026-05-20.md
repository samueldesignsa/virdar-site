A new patient walks out of your dental office after their first cleaning.

Good appointment. They say they'll see you in six months.

In those six months, they'll see a competitor's ad. A friend will recommend someone else. Their insurance changes. They move to a new neighborhood. Life interrupts.

The practice is counting on a recall reminder at month 5. One text. Maybe two.

What most offices don't have is anything that happens in the first 30 days — when the experience is still fresh, when they're deciding whether this is their dentist or just one they tried once.

A text the next day asking how the appointment went.
A message at week two confirming what's in their treatment plan.
A note at day 28 letting them know their chart is active and their next visit is on the calendar.

None of this is complicated. All of it is missing from most practices.

The businesses that retain the most clients don't have better services. They have a structured first-30-days sequence. The ones that don't — dental, fitness, PT, insurance, financial advisors — wait for the 6-month reminder and wonder why people drift.

Most businesses have no first-30-days sequence. That gap is measurable, and it's fixable.
