# LinkedIn Post — Wednesday, June 17, 2026

I walked into a dental office yesterday that has been at the same location for 22 years.

The owner had the numbers cold: how many patients he sees per month, average hygiene schedule, no-show rate. He ran a tight operation.

What he didn't have a number for was patients who quietly drifted out. People who came in 14 to 20 months ago and never rescheduled. Not because they switched dentists — because nobody reached them.

His estimate: around 200 names.

At this practice's average ticket, getting 60 of those patients back over the next six months is $82,000 in revenue from people he already had. No new marketing spend. No advertising. A message to people who liked the practice enough to come in and fell through the gap in the follow-up.

A 22-year practice doesn't have a marketing problem. It has a follow-up gap with a clear dollar figure attached to it.

The number is what gets a business owner's attention. Not the technology. The number.
