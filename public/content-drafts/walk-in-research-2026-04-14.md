## High-Probability Cold Targets (walk the streets in these areas)

These are business types commonly found in Oak Cliff and Lakewood. Look for the signals listed.

### 3. Any Family-Owned HVAC Company (Oak Cliff / South Dallas)
- **Where to find:** Drive Zang Blvd, Jefferson Blvd, and Cockrell Hill Rd. Small HVAC companies often have a house-converted-to-office or a plain storefront with a truck parked out front.
- **Signals to look for:** Wrapped truck in the lot, no appointment booking link on their website (or no website at all), Google listing with 15–40 reviews and no/few owner responses.
- **Best hook:** Missed call recovery. Ask how they handle calls when their tech is on a job.
- **Walk-in line:** "I work with home service companies on the automation side — specifically around missed calls and lead follow-up. Do you have 5 minutes?"

### 4. Any Independent Pest Control Company (South Dallas / Cockrell Hill)
- **Where to find:** Check Google Maps for "pest control" in Oak Cliff — filter by "locally owned." Look for businesses with fewer than 50 reviews and a phone-only contact method.
- **Signals to look for:** Seasonal spike business. Spring is their busiest season (right now). If they're busy, they're missing calls. If they're slow, they need follow-up on quotes.
- **Best hook:** Missed call recovery (spring is prime time — people see roaches after rain, they call immediately, they don't leave voicemails). 
- **Walk-in line:** "Do you have a lot of people calling and hanging up without leaving a message? What do you do with those?"

### 5. Any Electrician (Lakewood / East Dallas)
- **Where to find:** Gaston Ave, Abrams Rd, Mockingbird Ln. Solo or 2-tech electrical companies.
- **Signals to look for:** One-man shop, calls go straight to cell, no scheduling system visible, Google listing with under 20 reviews.
- **Best hook:** Estimate follow-up. Electricians do free estimates constantly. The ones they don't close sit and die. A follow-up sequence on open estimates alone is worth the conversation.
- **Walk-in line:** "I build automated follow-up systems for contractors. What percentage of the estimates you send actually get accepted?"
