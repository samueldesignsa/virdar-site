### 2. Independent Med Spa — Henderson Ave
**Address:** Henderson Ave corridor between Glencoe and Worth St, Dallas 75206
**What they do:** Aesthetics practice — injectables (Botox, fillers), laser, facials. 1-2 providers. Knox-Henderson has 4-6 independent med spas that serve the Uptown/Lower Greenville affluent residential market.
**What I noticed:** Med spas in this corridor have Instagram followings in the 2,000 to 12,000 range and post aggressively — promotions, before/after, seasonal offers. But their follow-up infrastructure is almost zero. A client who came in for a $400 Botox treatment in February has not received a single message from the practice telling them their results are wearing off and it's time to book again. Botox wears off in 3-4 months — that's a predictable reactivation window that most independent med spas completely ignore.
**Best hook:** Lapsed client reactivation with treatment interval timing. "Your clients who had Botox in February or March are at the 4-month mark right now — that's when results start to fade. Do you have a system that reaches them at that exact window, or does it depend on them remembering to call?" The answer is always "depends on them."
**Revenue math:** 40 lapsed injectable clients at 4-month interval × 55% response × $380 average treatment = $8,360 from one reactivation sequence. Ongoing monthly reactivation adds $1,200-2,000/month recurring.
**Leave-behind:** virdar.co/blog/ai-automation-for-med-spas-aesthetic-clinics.html

**What they do:** Chiropractic or integrative wellness practice, 1-2 providers. Henderson Ave has a cluster of these near the Belmont Hotel strip — they serve the Uptown/Lower Greenville residential population.

### 5. Independent Physical Therapy Practice — Uptown / McKinney Ave
**Address:** McKinney Ave corridor, Dallas 75204 — multiple independent PT practices between Ross Ave and Lemmon Ave
**What they do:** Outpatient physical therapy, 1-2 therapists, private practice (not hospital-affiliated). Uptown has a cluster of these serving the young professional population that's active in running and fitness.
**What I noticed:** PT practices in Uptown have a specific lapse problem that's different from chiro: patients who complete a treatment episode (typically 8-12 visits) are never reactivated for maintenance or injury prevention. The patient who finished ACL rehab in March is not getting a call about summer running injury prevention or back-to-training maintenance. Are you sending anything to patients after they discharge, or does it go quiet?" It always goes quiet.
**Revenue math:** 30 post-episode patients × 40% response × $650 average return episode = $7,800 from one reactivation sequence.

## Zone Notes

**Knox-Henderson on a Friday morning is accessible.** Owners are in, the lunch rush hasn't started, and business owners in Uptown are generally more open to quick conversations than the suburban practices Beverly has been hitting. The bar to conversation is lower — but so is the average deal size. These are younger practices, smaller client bases. The upside is faster conversion cycle and a higher concentration of businesses per block.
