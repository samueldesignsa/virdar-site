## One Insight

The Garland/Mesquite market is worth more walk-in time than Beverly has spent in it.

The Allen/McKinney zone is good — high-income, high-growth, sophisticated owners who understand the value of systems. But the auto repair shops, plumbers, and HVAC companies in Garland are a different buyer. They're not waiting for the right pitch. They're in pain right now — the service advisor's desk has a stack of declined services from last week, the maintenance reminder list hasn't been touched, and the owner is doing estimates, dispatching, and following up on unpaid invoices all at the same time. The problem is obvious and immediate.

The pitch for an independent shop owner in Garland doesn't need to be polished. It needs to be specific. "You have 30 declined services from last month sitting in Mitchell1. I can build you a system that follows up on every one of them automatically. Here's what the math looks like." That's the conversation.

The $15,000–$18,000 price point is also a stronger fit for a trades business owner who thinks in terms of what a truck costs, what a tech costs, and what a slow month costs — not in terms of software subscriptions. Frame the investment against what a single slow week costs in lost revenue, not against what the software does.
