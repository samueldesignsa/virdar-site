### 1. Independent Physical Therapy Practice (Frisco / North Plano)
- **Where to look:** Search "physical therapy Frisco TX" or "physical therapy North Plano" on Google Maps. Look for independently branded practices — not large hospital-affiliated PT chains (Baylor Scott & White, Texas Health). Target practices with 30 to 150 Google reviews and 1 to 3 therapists.
- **What they do:** Post-surgical rehab, sports injury, chronic pain management, workers' comp cases. Mostly insurance-billed with some cash-pay. Average clinical episode: 10 to 18 sessions over 6 to 10 weeks.
- **What to notice before walking in:** Check their Google Maps listing — how many reviews, and how recent are the most recent ones? A practice with 60 reviews but nothing posted in the last 4 months has a review request gap. Also check: does their website have online scheduling, or does it say "call to schedule"? Call-to-schedule PT practices have a more manual operations profile and are better targets.

### 2. Independent Chiropractic Practice (Frisco / North Plano)
- **Where to look:** Search "chiropractor Frisco TX" or "chiropractor Preston Road Plano." Look for single-provider or two-provider independent practices with 50 to 200 Google reviews. Avoid franchise wellness brands (The Joint Chiropractic) — the decision-maker at a franchise location isn't the person you need.
- **What they do:** Spinal adjustments, decompression, sports chiropractic, maintenance care plans. High repeat-visit dependency — most successful chiro practices run patients on monthly maintenance plans after the acute phase resolves. If those maintenance patients lapse, the practice loses recurring revenue it was counting on.
- **What to notice:** Check whether they offer care plans or packages on their website. A practice selling 12-visit packages has a patient compliance problem if patients buy the package and only use 4 sessions. Also look at their review response rate on Google — most chiro practices don't respond to their reviews.
- **Best hook:** Maintenance plan compliance and lapsed patient reactivation. "What percentage of your maintenance plan patients actually come in on schedule? And what happens to the ones who drop off after the acute phase?"
- **Conversation opener:** "I build retention systems for chiropractic practices. The maintenance plan patients who come in once a month are your most valuable clients — and the ones most likely to quietly lapse when life gets busy. I build the system that sends a reminder 10 days before they're due, with a direct scheduling link. Practices that run this see a 20 to 30 percent improvement in maintenance plan retention. Want to see the math?"

- **What they do:** Preventive care, wellness exams, vaccinations, sick visits, dental cleanings, surgery. Revenue model is appointment-based with a strong preventive care schedule — annual wellness exams, tri-annual dental check-ins, vaccine boosters on specific schedules.

Look at their website for a patient portal — most independent vet clinics use basic scheduling software (AVImark, Cornerstone, IDEXX) without any automated recall system. Also check review count and recency — a clinic with 200 reviews that hasn't gotten a new one in 60 days has a review request gap.
- **Best hook:** Annual wellness recall and vaccine reminder automation. "For a dog that came in for a wellness exam 11 months ago — what's your system for making sure the owner brings them back before the year is up?"

- **What they do:** Small-group and private classes, class packs, memberships. Revenue leak: people who buy a 10-class pack, use 3, and let the rest expire. Or annual members who stop coming in March but don't cancel until June.

- **Best hook:** Class pack utilization and lapsed member re-engagement. "What happens when someone buys a 10-class pack and only uses 3 of them? Does your system flag that and reach out, or do they just expire?"

Not large national firms (Edward Jones, H&R Block). The independently owned RIA or CPA practice has full control over their technology and client communication.
