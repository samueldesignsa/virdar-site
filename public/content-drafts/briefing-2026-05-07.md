## One Insight

Insurance agencies are a better walk-in target than the initial GTM suggested.

The obvious concern: insurance agents are trained salespeople. They know every sales move, they deflect pitches reflexively, and they're skeptical of anyone selling them something new. That's true. But there's a specific conversation that bypasses the sales defensiveness entirely: lead with their clients, not with the product.

"Tell me about the last renewal you lost that surprised you. A client who you thought was fine and then didn't renew." Almost every independent agent has a story. That story is the opening. Because the loss wasn't about the coverage. It wasn't about the rate. It was because nobody called at the right time.

Once they've told the story, the conversation isn't about selling them automation. It's about preventing that story from happening again. "What if the system had sent a message from you 30 days before that renewal — specific to their policy, from your email address — asking if they had any concerns?" That's not a technology pitch. That's a "what if the thing you already believe in (relationships) actually happened consistently" pitch.

The close in this market is easier than it looks from the outside.
