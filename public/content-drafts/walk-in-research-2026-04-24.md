### 4. High-End Med Spa or Aesthetics Practice on Preston Rd or Lovers Lane
- **Address:** Search "med spa Preston Road Dallas" or "aesthetics clinic Highland Park" on Google Maps. Look for independently owned practices with 50 to 300 reviews, no online booking visible on their website.
- **What they do:** Injectables, laser treatments, skin care. Average ticket $400–$1,500 per visit. Highly appointment-dependent. High sensitivity to no-shows and cancellations because provider time is the scarce resource.
- **What I noticed:** Med spas in Park Cities and Preston Center serve an affluent demographic that books based on trust and convenience. The practices that make it easiest to book — and that send the most reliable reminders — have lower no-show rates and higher rebooking rates. Most med spas are still running on phone-based scheduling with email confirmations that go unread.
- **Best hook:** Automated appointment reminders and rebooking sequences. "What's your no-show rate, and what do you do with patients who haven't rebooked in 90 days?"
- **Conversation opener:** "I build reminder and rebooking systems for med spas and aesthetics practices. Do you have 5 minutes?"

### 5. Independent Insurance Agency Near Preston Center
- **Address:** Search "independent insurance agent Preston Center Dallas" or "insurance agency near Northwest Highway Dallas." Look for State Farm, Allstate, Farmers agents — they're independently operated franchise owners, not employees.
- **What they do:** Personal and commercial lines insurance. Renewal-driven business with a large book of existing clients. Annual renewal is the moment clients either stay or shop around.
- **What I noticed:** Independent insurance agents who operate franchise offices (State Farm, Allstate, etc.) are small business owners who own their book. Retention is everything — losing 10% of a book per year to competitors compounds badly. Most agents send a renewal letter and hope for the best. An automated renewal sequence that starts 60 days out and includes a personal touchpoint from the agent dramatically reduces policy lapses and competitive shopping.
- **Best hook:** Renewal retention and cross-sell sequences. "When a policy comes up for renewal, what's your process for making sure the client doesn't shop around? And how many of your clients have only one policy with you when they could have two or three?"
- **Conversation opener:** "I work with insurance agencies on the retention and cross-sell side. Most agents with a 500-client book are losing 40 to 50 clients per year to competitors — not because they're unhappy, but because nobody reminded them of the relationship at renewal time. I build the sequence that does that automatically. Do you have 5 minutes?"
