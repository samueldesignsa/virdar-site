## One Insight

Optometry is the best vertical Virdar hasn't pitched yet, for one specific reason: the benefit expiration campaign.

Every other automation pitch is about recovering something gradual — lapsed patients who drifted, missed follow-ups that accumulate over months. The optometry benefit expiration campaign is about a hard deadline. December 31. The patient's $200 frame allowance disappears at midnight. That's not a soft opportunity — it's a ticking clock.

The pitch writes itself: "You have 600 patients on calendar-year vision plans. Their frame and lens benefits expire December 31. How many of them know that right now, in May, and have already booked their fall exam? A few. What happens in October if every one of those 600 patients gets a specific message — 'Your VSP benefit includes a $200 frame allowance that expires December 31, here's a link to schedule' — timed to when they still have enough runway to get an appointment? Most of them come in."

That's not automation as a nice-to-have. That's automation capturing insurance money that is literally expiring unused. The OD doesn't have to convince anyone to buy something. They just have to tell patients their own benefit is about to disappear.

For Beverly's pitch: lead with the calendar. "What does your Q4 look like right now? Are you filling November and December proactively with benefit expiration outreach, or are you waiting to see what comes in?" Any OD who's ever had empty chairs in November while knowing patients had unused benefits will want to fix that. The fix is one campaign. The campaign is one system.
