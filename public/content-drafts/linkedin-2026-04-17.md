# LinkedIn Post — Friday, April 17, 2026

Someone fills out a contact form on your website. They have a problem. They want to talk.

Research has shown this consistently: the odds of converting that person drop by 80% after the first 5 minutes. After an hour, you're ten times less likely to close them than if you'd responded immediately.

This isn't about being pushy. It's about timing. The person filled out that form while the problem was front of mind. Five minutes later, life moved on. By morning, they've already talked to someone else.

The businesses that win on web inquiries aren't the ones with the best salespeople. They're the ones with the fastest first response — even if it's just a confirmation and a booking link sent automatically within 60 seconds.

You're not replacing the sales conversation. You're making sure you get to have it.

Every form submission that sits in an inbox overnight is a conversation that probably won't happen. The fix is straightforward. Most businesses just haven't built it yet.
