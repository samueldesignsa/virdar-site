# LinkedIn Post — Monday, June 1, 2026

A painting contractor in North Texas quoted 23 exterior jobs this spring.

He followed up on 6 of them — the ones where the homeowner specifically asked him to check back.

The other 17 estimates: he sent the quote, waited, and figured no news meant they went with someone else.

I talked to three of those 17 homeowners separately. Two were still deciding in late April. One had just forgotten to call back.

All three ended up booking other painters who sent a second message.

Not a better price. Not better work. Just a message that showed up when the homeowner was finally ready to move.

If you quote 20 jobs this season and close 7, the other 13 aren't all "no." Some are "not yet." Some are sitting in an email folder waiting for a prompt.

The painters who figure this out outperform their market by 15 to 20 points on close rate — from the same leads, with the same overhead.
